When reverse-engineering winners and top-3 finishers across the entire Greyville
Polytrack dataset, three highly repeatable, non-odds, non-zero metrics emerge:

1.  The Turning Radius Geometric Constraint (R_{limit}): On the sweeping turns

    displacement > 3\text{m} from the rail) over distances under 1400m incurs a

Part 4: Decision Tree Analysis, Gaps & Programmatic Corrections

Gaps and Errors in the Original Logic:

1.  Overestimating "Slick Glide" on Outside Lanes: We assumed the light rain
    would lubricate the outer strip. In reality, light rain compacts the
    synthetic wax along the rail, creating a highly efficient, low-friction
    inside lane.
2.  Somatic Maturity Sieve Error: The model over-penalized the 2yo Preacher Man.
    While she lacked somatic muscle density compared to 5yo mares, the dramatic
    Class Drop (from Listed/sales feature races to Class 5) was a more dominant
    vector than physical maturity.

New Programmatic Formulas to Correct the Logic:

Formula 1: Corrected Centrifugal Lateral Tax (CCLT)

We must integrate a dynamic lateral tax to modify the baseline speed of wide
runners.
CCLT = C_d \times \left( \frac{V^2}{R_{turn}} \right) \times \sin(\theta_{draw})
Where:

  - C_d (Drag Coefficient) = 0.15 for synthetic surfaces.
  - R_{turn} = Turn radius (140\text{m}).
  - \theta_{draw} = Radial gate angle (calculated as \text{Draw} \times 0.08
    radians).
  - Application: This subtracts velocity directly from the predicted top speed
    of runners drawn outside Gate 8 on tight turns.

Formula 2: Somatic Class Override (SCO)

We resolve the somatic age penalty by scaling it against the class drop
magnitude.
SCO = \mu_{age} \times \left( 1 - \frac{\Delta Class}{Class_{max}} \right)
Where:

  - \mu_{age} = somatic penalty (0.08 for 2yos against older).
  - \Delta Class = number of class levels dropped (4 levels in Preacher Man's
    case).
  - Application: This reduces the age penalty to near-zero when a class drop is
    substantial, correctly keeping horses like Preacher Man in the Top-3
    calculations.

Formula 3: Moisture Compaction Index (MCI)

This adjusts track friction along the rail under wet weather conditions.
MCI = \mu_{base} - (\rho_{moisture} \times \beta_{rail\_density}) Where:

  - \rho_{moisture} = rain intensity (0.2 for light rain).
  - \beta_{rail\_density} = rail compaction factor (0.8 for synthetic wax).
  - Application: When MCI decreases, the friction coefficient on the inside rail
    drops, meaning the geometric advantage of Barrier 1 is amplified.
