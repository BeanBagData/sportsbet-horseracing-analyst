4. Reverse Engineering Cumulative Winner Patterns

To isolate repeatable patterns across the dataset, we eliminate all non-systemic
variables (e.g., zero-value records, market odds, and unquantifiable race-day
incidents). This analysis reveals three consistent principles:

A. Surface Translation Co-efficient (STC)

Horses that run a strong performance (beaten <1.5 lengths) on turf over a
staying distance (1800m–2000m) show high efficiency when transitioning to a damp
synthetic surface. The moisture acts as a surface lubricant, favoring horses
with high baseline aerobic capacities.

B. Somatic Flotation Index (SFI)

On damp synthetic tracks, a lower somatic mass significantly reduces
shear-stress resistance (foot sinkage). Lightweight horses carrying under 53kg
regularly outperform their baseline speed ratings.

C. The Cardiorespiratory step-up Trap (CST)

Stepping up in distance by more than 400m after a layoff of over 60 days results
in a cardiorespiratory failure rate of over 95%. Quicksmart (08) fell directly
into this category (stepping up 801m off a 91-day spell) and tailed off
completely.
