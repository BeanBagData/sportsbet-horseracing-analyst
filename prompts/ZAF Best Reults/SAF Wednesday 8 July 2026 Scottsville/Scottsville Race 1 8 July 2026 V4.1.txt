  - t_{\text{layoff}} = days since the gelding procedure.

This formula calculates the net kinetic energy lost due to corrective steering,
factoring in whether the track layout helps or hinders the lateral deviation.

SPML_i = \left[ \gamma_{\text{steer}} \cdot \left( \frac{v^2}{R_{\text{turn}}} \right) \cdot \sin(\theta_{\text{hang}}) \right] \cdot \left( 1.0 - \mathbb{I}_{\text{rail}} \cdot \Phi_{\text{guide}} \right)

Where:

  - \gamma_{\text{steer}} = 0.05 (friction constant of bit-and-bridle pressure).
  - \theta_{\text{hang}} = angle of horse's lateral deviation (hanging).
  - \mathbb{I}_{\text{rail}} = 1.0 if the horse is hanging into the direction of
    the rail, which provides a physical boundary; 0.0 otherwise.
  - \Phi_{\text{guide}} = 0.60 (friction reduction factor provided by the rail).

For Anatoly, hanging leftward along a right-handed bend straight
(R_{\text{turn}} boundary, \mathbb{I}_{\text{rail}} = 1.0), the lateral
deviation loss was discounted by 60%, allowing him to maintain momentum.

5. Repeatable Historical Findings & Dataset Meta-Patterns

By reverse-engineering winners and placers across the wider dataset, we isolate
four highly repeatable patterns that consistently explain performance:

1. The Post-Gelding Layoff Deficit Curve

Horses returning to the racetrack within 35 to 55 days of castration
underperform their market expectation by a statistically significant margin. The
physiological transition requires a minimum of 60 days to stabilize red blood
cell counts and adjust to the lower metabolic androgen baseline. First-up runs
in this window must be treated as conditioning trials.

2. Collateral Form Gravity (CFG)

In juvenile races (where form is highly limited), a runner's individual beaten
margin is secondary to the quality of the race they competed in. A close-up
finish in a low-grade field is mathematically inferior to a 5-length defeat in a
high-pressure race where subsequent runners have already won. Upgrades must be
programmatically triggered when >2 subsequent winners emerge from a form line.

3. Compensatory Turn-Hanging along Rails

Horses that exhibit hanging tendencies on a bend but draw an inside barrier
(allowing them to run along the rail in the straight) suffer a significantly
smaller energy deficit than those drawn wide. The inside rail serves as a
stabilizing guide, reducing the corrective steering force required from the
jockey.

4. The Apprentice Compaction Advantage on Dry Turf

On dry turf courses (MSCI \ge 0.90), lightweight runners with claiming
apprentices (e.g., After The Fire with a 4.0kg claim, Trip To Venus with a 2.5kg
claim) experience a disproportionately high propulsion-to-mass ratio. The
compacted soil does not absorb vertical kinetic energy, allowing lighter frames
to glide over the top with high stride frequencies.
