Phase 8: Supreme Thermodynamic Archetypes

The first Tier 1 pattern that fires becomes the Locked Engine. In the event of
co-firing archetypes on synthetic courses, the conflict is resolved strictly by
track surface temperature: if T_{\text{track}} < 16.0^\circ\text{C}, the
hardened wax-sand matrix patterns take absolute precedence; if
T_{\text{track}} \ge 16.0^\circ\text{C}, the viscoelastic suction patterns take
absolute precedence.

1.  Iso-Kinetic Sparing (IKS) Protocol: Triggered on firm tracks
    (\text{MSCI}_{\text{eff}, i} \ge 0.90). Favours low-weight, inside-drawn
    frontrunners who save maximum ground.
2.  Financial Class Gravity Lock (PPGI Supremacy): Triggers when a horse's
    prize-money index exceeds the field average by \ge 3.0 standard deviations.
3.  Geometric Slipstream Lock (Marathon Draft): For staying distances
    \ge 2400\text{ m}, locks the most efficient drafting runner behind a
    high-tempo pace leader.
4.  Thermodynamic Collapse: Triggers when the temperature exceeds 35°C on heavy
    turf, forcing the collapse of high-weight frontrunners.
5.  Newtonian Slingshot (Handicap Mass Wave): Triggers on Soft 6+ tracks.
    Favours runners carrying \le 53.0\text{ kg} mapping on the fresh outside
    lanes.
6.  Aerobic Grind: Triggers in high-tempo marathons, locking stayers with STR
    \ge 0.80.
7.  Youth & Maturation Supremacy: For 3YO-only developmental fields, locks
    late-maturing runners with dominant second-up trial peaks.
8.  Staying Maiden Pivot: In staying maidens, locks fit, experienced local
    stayers over unexposed debutants.
