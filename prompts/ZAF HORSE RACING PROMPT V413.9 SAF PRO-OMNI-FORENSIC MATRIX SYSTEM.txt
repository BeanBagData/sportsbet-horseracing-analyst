    the current system date/time and the local scheduled jump time:
    \Delta t_{\text{shift}} = t_{\text{jump}} - t_{\text{system}} \quad (\text{hours})

Step 0.31: Dynamic Temporal Soil Wetting/Hardening Projection

  - If \Delta t_{\text{shift}} > 0 and weather forecasts project active rainfall
    between t_{\text{system}} and t_{\text{jump}}, apply the wetting phase model

    \text{MSCI}_{\text{jump}} = \text{MSCI}_{\text{base}} \times e^{-\lambda_{\text{wet}} \cdot \Delta t_{\text{shift}}}

    downpours [2].

    T_{\text{air}} \ge 30^\circ\text{C} and zero rain, apply the
    drying/hardening model:
    \text{MSCI}_{\text{jump}} = \min\left(1.0, \ \text{MSCI}_{\text{base}} + \lambda_{\text{dry}} \cdot \Delta t_{\text{shift}}\right)
    Where \lambda_{\text{dry}} = 0.02 per hour.
  - Use \text{MSCI}_{\text{jump}} as the primary soil compaction index
    (\text{MSCI}) in all downstream thermodynamic and drag calculations.

1.  STR (Somatic-Twitch Ratio — Muscle Profile):
    \text{STR}_i = \left(\frac{\text{Sire\_AWD}}{1200}\right) \times \left(1.0 - \text{SDF}_i\right)
    Measures the ratio of slow-twitch muscle fibres to fast-twitch power. High
    STR dictates staying and heavy-ground efficiency.
2.  VERI (Viscoelastic Energy Retention Index — Spleen):
    \text{VERI}_i = E_{\text{Tank},i} \times \left(1.0 - C_{\text{visco},i}\right)
    Isolates the latent oxygenated red blood cell battery capacity remaining
    after overcoming track friction.
3.  CML (Class-to-Mass Leverage — Cardiac Output):
    \text{CML}_i = \frac{\text{Raw\_NLS}_i}{\nu_{\text{base}} \times \left(W_{\text{eff}} - 54.0\right)}
    Measures left ventricle capacity under mass loads; high CML preserves
    aerobic pacing without early lactic build-up.
4.  TCF (Thermoregulatory Cooling Factor — Latherin):
    \mu_{\text{temp}} = \mu_{\text{base}} \times \left(1.0 + 0.04 \times \max\left(0, T_{\text{air}} - 15.0\right) \times \left(\frac{H_{\text{rel}}}{100}\right)\right)
    Calibrates sweat-detergent cooling drag. If
    T_{\text{air}} < 15^\circ\text{C}, the cooling system is uninhibited,
    zeroing out temperature drag surcharges.
5.  RVI (Respiratory Volume Index — Airway Capacity):
    \Delta\nu_{\text{gear\_airway}} = \text{Airway Assist Gear Flags} Adjusts
    the frailty coefficient based on corrective airway gear (e.g., first-time
    Tongue Ties or Cross-Over Nosebands) to maintain maximum lung ventilation.

Section 5: Calibrated Biophysical & Veterinary Protocols

1.  Root-Mat Tensile Cohesion Benefit (\text{ITC}_i):
    \text{ITC}_i = 1.0 - \beta_{\text{mat}} \times \sum_{j < i} \left(1.0 - \gamma_{\text{lateral}, ij}\right)
    Where \beta_{\text{mat}} = 0.08 is the turf shear factor, and
    \gamma_{\text{lateral}, ij} is the lateral deviation coordinate. For the
    leader, \text{ITC}_{\text{leader}} = 1.0. For followers, it degrades base
    soil stiffness: k_{\text{s}, i} = k_{\text{s, pristine}} \times \text{ITC}_i
2.  Viscoelastic Soil-Penetration Mass Tax (z_{\text{pen}, i}):
    z_{\text{pen}, i} = \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} \times \text{PLW}_{\text{factor}, i}}{\Phi_{\text{soil}} \times A_{\text{hoof}}} \right) \times \left(1.0 + \beta_{\text{track}} \times \left[1.0 - \text{MSCI}\right]\right)
    Where \Phi_{\text{soil}} is the base soil shear strength (1.2 for pristine
    turf, 0.8 for rain-affected turf where \text{MSCI} \le 0.80),
    A_{\text{hoof}} = 120\text{ cm}^2, and \beta_{\text{track}} is the
    compaction lane wear modifier (1.0 for clean lanes, 2.5 for worn inside
    lanes). If z_{\text{pen}, i} \ge 2.5\text{ cm}, calculate Slippage
    Coefficient
    \mu_{\text{eff}, i} = 1.0 - 0.15 \times (z_{\text{pen}, i} - 2.0) and apply
    to propulsive force:
    F_{\text{prop, adj}, i} = F_{\text{prop}, i} \times \mu_{\text{eff}, i}
3.  Cardiorespiratory Detraining Index (\text{CRDI}_i):
    \text{CRDI}_i = \delta_{\text{2nd-up}, i} \times e^{-\frac{\Delta t_{\text{lay}, i}}{100}} \times \left( \frac{\text{Margin}_{\text{1st-up}, i}}{1.0 + \text{Days}_{\text{between}, i}} \right) \times \xi_{\text{surface}}
    Where \delta_{\text{2nd-up}, i} = 1.0 if the runner is 2nd-up off a layoff
    of \ge 75 days (else 0.0), \Delta t_{\text{lay}, i} is spell length,
    \text{Margin}_{\text{1st-up}, i} is first-up margin,
    \text{Days}_{\text{between}, i} is interval days, and \xi_{\text{surface}}
    is surface drag (1.0 for Firm/Good, 1.55 for Soft/Heavy turf).
4.  Sophomore Growth & Transition Scalar (S_{\text{sgt}}):
    S_{\text{sgt}, i} = \begin{cases} 1.0 + \alpha_{\text{growth}} \times \ln\left( \text{Month} - 2.0 \right) \times \left( \frac{58.0 - W_{\text{actual}, i}}{58.0} \right) & \text{if Age}_i == 2 \text{ and Class is Mixed-Age and Month} > 2 \\ 1.0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    Where \alpha_{\text{growth}} = 0.05.
5.  Jockey Restraint Risk Index (\text{JRRI}_i):
    \text{JRRI}_i = \begin{cases} 0.15 & \text{if KGS}_i \le 2.0 \text{ and Jockey\_Style}_i == \text{"Hold-Up"} \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    If \text{JRRI}_i = 0.15, reduce the metabolic energy tank capacity:
    E_{\text{Tank}, i} \leftarrow E_{\text{Tank}, i} \times (1.0 - \text{JRRI}_i)
6.  Gate Speed / Turn Drag Compactor (C_{\text{els}, i}):
    C_{\text{els}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.60 & \text{if Barrier}_{\text{actual}, i} \ge 7 \text{ and Track Turning } (R_{\text{turn}} < 120\text{ m}) \text{ and KGS}_i \le 1.8 \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}

PHASE 3.7: FORENSIC METRIC CONSOLIDATION (THE KINETIC ENGINE)

To prevent data leaks and maintain complete operational symmetry, all physical,
environmental, and spatial anomalies identified in the historical databases are
resolved through the unified formulas below:

  - Formula 1: Wet Rail Degradation Coefficient (\text{WRDC}_i)
    \text{WRDC}_i = \text{TRF} \times \left( \frac{k}{N_{\text{races}}} \right) \times (1.0 - \text{MSCI}) \times \left( \frac{1}{\text{Lane}_i} \right)
    Where TRF is the Track Rating Factor (Good 4 = 0.0; Soft 6 = 1.4; Heavy 9
    = 2.5). If \text{WRDC}_i \ge 1.50, Lanes 1–2 are designated "Compaction
    Bogs."
  - Formula 2: Dynamic Wetting vs. Saturated Index (\text{DWSI})
    \text{DWSI} = \frac{\Delta t_{\text{rain}}}{30} \cdot e^{-\lambda_{\text{saturation}} \cdot t_{\text{rain}}}
    Where t_{\text{rain}} is the duration of active rainfall (minutes), and
    \lambda_{\text{saturation}} = 0.035 for turf. If
    0.10 \le \text{DWSI} \le 0.45, activate "Slick Glide" status; if
    \text{DWSI} > 0.45, activate "Saturated Suction" status.
  - Formula 3: Synthetic Viscoelastic Drag (D_{\text{syn\_visco}, i})
    D_{\text{syn\_visco}, i} = \mu_{\text{temp}} \times \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} - 54.0}{54.0} \right) \times \left( 1.0 - \alpha_{\text{rain}} \cdot \text{Moisture} \right)
    \mu_{\text{temp}} = \mu_{\text{base}} \times \left(1.0 + \beta_{\text{visco}} \cdot \max(0, T_{\text{air}} - 15.0)\right)
    Where \mu_{\text{base}} = 0.08, \beta_{\text{visco}} = 0.04, and
    \alpha_{\text{rain}} = 0.15.
  - Formula 4: Camber-vs-Viscoelastic Traction Index (\text{CVTI}_i)
    \text{CVTI}_i = \left( \frac{R_{\text{turn}}}{L_{\text{straight}}} \right) \cdot \left( \text{Lane}_i - 1 \right) \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}) \cdot \beta_{\text{curve}}
    Where \beta_{\text{curve}} = 1.50 if R_{\text{turn}} < 80\text{ m}
    (Caulfield Heath, Doomben, Mildura, Devonport, Corowa); 1.00 if
    80\text{ m} \le R_{\text{turn}} \le 120\text{ m}; 0.50 if
    R_{\text{turn}} > 120\text{ m}.
  - Formula 5: Starting Gate Vacancy Coefficient (K_{\text{sgv}, i})
    K_{\text{sgv}, i} = 1.0 - \alpha_{\text{vacuum}} \cdot \sum_{j \in \{-1, 1\}} \left( \frac{\Omega_{\text{vacant}, i+j}}{j^2} \right)
    Where \alpha_{\text{vacuum}} = 0.075, and \Omega_{\text{vacant}} indicates
    empty adjacent stalls.
  - Formula 6: Pocketing Hazard Index (\text{PSI}_i)
    \text{PSI}_i = \left( 1.0 - \frac{R_{\text{turn}}}{C_{\text{track}}} \right) \cdot \ln\left( \delta_{\text{rail}} \cdot B_{\text{comp}} \right) \cdot \left[ 1.0 + \gamma_{\text{front}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{FP}_{\text{dens}}}{1.5} \right) \right]
    Where \gamma_{\text{front}} = 0.25, and \text{FP}_{\text{dens}} is the
    projected number of frontrunners inside Barrier 5.
  - Formula 7: Aerodynamic Drafting Coefficient (C_{\text{draft}, i})
    C_{\text{draft}, i} = 1.0 + \alpha_{\text{draft}} \cdot e^{-\beta_{\text{pos}} \cdot \left( \text{PSP}_i - 1.0 \right)} \cdot \left( 1.0 - \frac{\text{LSO}_i}{B_{\text{field}}} \right)
    Where \alpha_{\text{draft}} = 0.15 and \beta_{\text{pos}} = 0.5.
  - Formula 8: Centrifugal Lead Advantage (K_{\text{cla}, i})
    K_{\text{cla}, i} = \left( 1.0 - \frac{R_{\text{turn}}}{C_{\text{track}}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{L_{\text{straight}}}{D_{\text{total}}} \right) \cdot \left( 1.0 + \left( \text{LSO}_{\text{chaser}} - \text{LSO}_{\text{leader}} \right) \right)
  - Formula 9: Metabolic Desynchronisation Index (\text{MDI}_i)
    \text{MDI}_i = \left( \frac{\text{Spell\_Days}_i}{100} \right) \times (1.0 + \text{MSCI}) \times \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right) \times \left( \frac{L_{\text{target}}}{1000} \right)
  - Formula 10: Somatic Frame-to-Mass Index (\text{SFMI}_i)
    \text{SFMI}_i = \begin{cases} 1.0 & \text{if } \text{MSCI} \ge 0.70 \text{ or Frame is "Large"} \\ 1.0 - \left[ (1.0 - \text{MSCI}) \times \left( \frac{54.0 - W_{\text{eff}, i}}{10} \right) \times \xi_{\text{sex}} \right] & \text{if } \text{MSCI} < 0.70 \text{ and Frame is "Small/Mare"} \end{cases}
    Where \xi_{\text{sex}} = 1.25 for females, and 1.00 for males.
  - Formula 11: Staying Pedigree Override Coefficient (K_{\text{spo}, i})

  - Formula 12: Pathological Alveolar Recovery Index (P_{\text{ari}, i})

  - Formula 18: Second-Up Recovery Decay (\text{SURD}_i)

  - Formula 46: Viscoelastic Suction Drag Index (\text{VSDI}_i) Models dynamic,

    Where \text{Sire AWD\_Factor} = \frac{\text{Sire AWD}}{1600}. If

  - Formula 48: Jockey Intent & Private Facility Modifier (\text{JIFM}_i)

    Where \text{JWR}_{\text{jockey}, i} is the trailing 12-month local win rate

    \mu_{\text{eff}, i} = 1.0 - 0.15 \times \left( z_{\text{pen}, i} - 2.0 \right)

    F_{\text{prop, adj}, i} = F_{\text{prop}, i} \times \mu_{\text{eff}, i}
  - Formula 61: Root-Mat Tensile Cohesion Benefit (\text{ITC}_i)
    \text{ITC}_i = 1.0 - \beta_{\text{mat}} \times \sum_{j < i} \left(1.0 - \gamma_{\text{lateral}, ij}\right)
    Where \beta_{\text{mat}} = 0.08 is the turf shear factor, and

    runner i and runner j. For the leader, \text{ITC}_{\text{leader}} = 1.0.

PHASE 4: SOVEREIGN KINETIC INDEX (SKI) ENGINE

Section 1: Baseline Ingestion & Metrical Anchors

To prevent static form traps, all mathematical evaluations of raw capacity are
consolidated into the Sovereign Kinetic Index (SKI). This continuous efficiency
index acts as a baseline "Raw Engine Capacity" index (\text{SKI}_i), which is
subsequently scaled to compute the Net Thermodynamic Conservation Index
(\text{NTCI}_i) [17].

Section 2: Raw Nominal Latent Speed (Raw NLS)

Calculate individual base speed potential on a scale of 50 to 100+:

1.  Base Scalar: Initialize with a standard baseline of 80.
2.  Career Class Adjustments:
      - Add up to +10 based on career win/place percentages and class
        competitiveness.
      - Deduct up to -10 for high-mileage, non-competitive profiles (Win Rate
        <5%).
3.  Recent Performance Peak: Add up to +5 for strong closing sectionals or
    close-margin finishes (<1.5\text{L}) in higher grades.
4.  Ex-Import Class Drop Override: If an international import drops to
    provincial company:
      - Add a Class Parity Exemption Score
        (\text{CPX} = \text{Class Drop Delta}). If \text{CPX} \ge 2.50, add +5
        directly to Raw NLS.
5.  Synthetic-Turf Decoupling: If today's track is Synthetic (Silo D), decouple
    turf form entirely from the baseline speed calculation. Compute Raw NLS via
    a weighted average of synthetic-specific historic speed figures:
    \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{synthetic}} = \left(0.70 \times \text{NLS}_{\text{synthetic\_historic}}\right) + \left(0.30 \times \text{NLS}_{\text{recent\_overall}}\right)
    Do not penalise veteran horses for recent poor turf form if localised
    synthetic form is proven, as synthetic stride mechanics are highly
    specialised.
6.  Sophomore Stamina Stability (SSS) Class Override: If Age = 3YO AND target
    distance \ge 2000\text{m} AND track condition is Soft/Heavy
    (\text{MSCI} \le 0.72) AND the runner possesses a prior win at
    \ge 2000\text{m} on Soft/Heavy ground, strictly reduce any applied negative
    Class Gravity penalties by 40% (prioritising physical capacity over class
    history).
7.  Castration Hormonal Recoil Adjustment: Multiply Raw NLS by \text{PGRF}_i
    (Formula 53) to account for surgical endocrine fluctuations.
8.  Detraining Aerobic Decay Adjustment: Deduct \text{CRDI}_i (Formula 54)
    directly from Raw NLS to adjust for un-triaged cardiorespiratory detraining
    during spells:
    \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{calibrated}, i} = \text{Raw NLS}_i \times \text{PGRF}_i - \text{CRDI}_i

PHASE 4.5: THE NET THERMODYNAMIC CONSERVATION INDEX (NTCI)

To prevent data leaks and maintain complete predictive dominance, all horse
ratings and final selections are determined strictly by the Net Thermodynamic
Conservation Index (NTCI), which measures kinetic energy conservation. The
Sovereign Kinetic Index (SKI) serves only as the baseline "Raw Engine Capacity"
index, which is subsequently coupled with soil, spatial, and metabolic modifiers
[17].

Formula 1: NTCI Scoring Integration

For every active carded runner, calculate the final NTCI today:
\text{NTCI}_i = \text{SKI}_{\text{calibrated}, i} \times \Theta_{\text{traction}, i} \times \Phi_{\text{spatial}, i} \times \Gamma_{\text{metabolic}, i}

Where:

1.  \Theta_{\text{traction}, i} is the Dynamic Soil Traction Coefficient,
    mapping hoof-slip and soil mechanics:
    \Theta_{\text{traction}, i} = C_{\text{vh}, i} \cdot \text{ITC}_i - \left[ \kappa \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_{\text{eff}, i})^{1.8} \cdot \left(\frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{54.0}\right)^{1.8} \cdot \text{VSP}_i \cdot (1.0 - \text{DWSI}) \right] + \left( \text{SFMI}_i - 1.0 \right) + \left( 0.05 \cdot \chi_{\text{wtt}, i} \right) - \Delta\nu_{\text{app}, i} - C_{\text{wtf}, i} - C_{\text{sink}, i} - \left(0.15 \times \max\left(0.0, z_{\text{pen}, i} - 2.0\right)\right)
2.  \Phi_{\text{spatial}, i} is the Spatial Efficiency Factor, mapping turn
    centrifugal taxes and pocketing hazard parameters:
    \Phi_{\text{spatial}, i} = C_{\text{draft}, i} \times \left( 1.0 - \left(\text{CPT}_{\text{turn}, i} \cdot \text{TCCF}\right) \right) \times \left( 1.0 - \delta_{\text{pocket}} \cdot \text{PSI}_i \cdot \text{PIF} \right) - \text{LDCP}_i \times S_{\text{draft}, i} - \left(0.01 \times \text{UCLV}_{\text{adj}, i}\right)
3.  \Gamma_{\text{metabolic}, i} is the Metabolic Conservation Factor, mapping
    cardiorespiratory decay, physical mass-drag anomalies, and age-sex limits:
    \Gamma_{\text{metabolic}, i} = \text{SURD}_i \cdot \Psi_{\text{recovery}, i} - \text{VSS}_i - \left( \Delta\nu_{\text{decay\_refined}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \chi_{\text{trial}, i}) \right) - \Delta\nu_{\text{brake}, i} + \left( \text{MAC}_i - 1.0 \right) + \left( 0.05 \cdot \text{CMAR}_{\text{adj}, i} \right) - \text{FSHI}_i - \text{STFF}_i - \text{JRRI}_i + \left(0.10 \times \text{MCGO}_i\right)

Formula 2: Thermodynamic Kinetic Surplus (TKS)

Compute the normalised thermodynamic surplus today across the active cohort,
completely replacing old market-price and SKI-based surplus calculations [8]:
\text{TKS}_i = \frac{\text{NTCI}_i - \overline{\text{NTCI}}_{\text{field}}}{\sigma_{\text{NTCI}}}
Where \overline{\text{NTCI}}_{\text{field}} represents the mean NTCI of the
active starting field, and \sigma_{\text{NTCI}} is the standard deviation of the
NTCI of the active starting field.
