2. Field Compression, Weight Correction & Degeneracy Rules

1.  Filter Scratchings: Remove all runners in race r where status is equal to
    "Scr" or "Scratched".
2.  Determine Active Field Density (N_{\text{active}, r}): Count the remaining
    active runners in race r.
3.  Determine Field Compression Ratio: Calculate the percentage of
    scratching-induced field contraction:
    \text{Contraction}_r = \frac{N_{\text{original}, r} - N_{\text{active}, r}}{N_{\text{original}, r}}
4.  Recalculate Starting Barriers (B_{\text{actual}, i}): Sort active runners by
    their original_barrier in ascending order. Assign actual starting barriers
    B_{\text{actual}, i} sequentially from 1 to N_{\text{active}, r} based on
    this sorted order.
      - Dynamic Post-Scratching Gate Compression (PSGC) Override: If the field
        compresses by \ge 25\% (\text{Contraction}_r \ge 0.25), all
        barrier-based "Barrier Death" vetoes (originally applied to wide
        barriers B_{\text{original}, i} \ge 12) must be dynamically suspended if
        the runner's recalculated starting barrier B_{\text{actual}, i} is
        \le 10. The runner is fully restored to the active pool.
5.  Field Compression Pace Factor (\Phi_{\text{FC}}): To prevent over-penalising
    backmarkers in contracted fields, calculate:
    \Phi_{\text{FC}, i} = \left( \frac{N_{\text{active}, r}}{N_{\text{original}, r}} \right)^{1.5}
    For runners mapped as backmarkers, scale down their spatial penalty:
    P_{\text{spatial}, i}^* = P_{\text{spatial}, i} \cdot \Phi_{\text{FC}, i}
6.  Apprentice Correction Heuristic (ACH): Check if apprentice_claim_kg is
    listed as 0.0 or is identical across all runners. Scan the jockey text
    string. If the name matches an apprentice registered on provincial or
    metropolitan circuits, manually apply their real weight claim:
    \text{ACH}_i = \begin{cases} 3.0\text{ kg} & \text{if jockey is "Holly Durnan" or "Logan Bates" or "Kirra-Lee Lane"} \\ 2.0\text{ kg} & \text{if jockey is "Dakotah Keane" or "Brittany Button" or "Jackson Radley" or "Rose Hammond" or "Christopher Pang"} \\ 1.5\text{ kg} & \text{if jockey is "Mollie Fitzgerald" or "Siena Grima" or "Caitlin Tootell" or "Cassidy Hill" or "Sam Kennedy"} \\ 0.0\text{ kg} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}

    W_{\text{eff}, i} = W_{\text{carried\_weight\_kg}, i} - \text{ACH}_i

    \text{CRMS}_i = \text{Eliminate if } \text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i} > 4.5\text{L} \quad \text{or} \quad \max(\text{Pos}_{t-1, i}, \text{Pos}_{t-2, i}, \text{Pos}_{t-3, i}) > 6.0 \cdot (1.0 - \mathbb{I}_{\text{Resuming}, i})

Stage 3: Pedigree-to-Distance Suitability Sieve

  - Rule: For sprint races (<1200\text{m}) on synthetic tracks, calculate the
    Pedigree Distance Variance (\Delta_{\text{AWD}}):
    \Delta_{\text{AWD}} = | \text{Sire AWD} - \text{Race Distance} | Eliminate
    any first-starter or unexposed runner (N_{\text{starts}} \le 2) contesting a
    short sprint where the Sire AWD exceeds 1800\text{m}. Such runners lack the
    physical fast-twitch muscle fibre development required to sprint synthetic
    distances.

Stage 4: Synthetic Aptitude Sieve (SAS)

  - Rule: On synthetic/polytrack surfaces, a runner must possess a Career
    Synthetic Place Rate (\text{SPR}) of \ge 15\% to qualify for the Top 4,
    unless the runner is a debutant (N_{\text{starts}} = 0) or has contested
    fewer than 2 synthetic starts.
  - Formula:
    \text{SPR}_i = \frac{\text{Synthetic Places}_i}{\max(1, \text{Synthetic Starts}_i)}
    \text{SAS}_i = \text{Eliminate if } \text{SPR}_i < 0.15 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Synthetic\_Starts} \ge 2}

Phase 2: Dynamic Latent Frailty (\nu_i) and NLS Calibration

Step A: Speed Baseline (NLS) Calibration

00. Deterministic Signature Pattern (DSP) Evaluation

Before executing standard speed baseline calibration, evaluate each active
runner against the following four physical, environmental, and stable-intent
Deterministic Signature Patterns (DSPs). If a runner matches the boundary
conditions, apply the hard-coded baseline speed and frailty adjustments
directly, overriding standard decay functions:

  - DSP-1 (The Local Viscoelastic Habituation Loop - Synthetic Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Target track is a synthetic polytrack surface
        (\mathbb{I}_{\text{Synthetic\_Active}} = 1), runner is trained on-course
        (\Theta_{\text{home}} = 0.75), and career synthetic place rate \ge 50\%
        over a minimum of 2 starts.
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 3.50 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.25.
  - DSP-2 (The Compaction-Weight Decoupling Curve - Wet Turf/Clay Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Wet turf track compaction
        (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.65), dynamic weight-elasticity exponent
        \gamma_{\text{elastic}} \ge 1.45, and the runner carries a lightweight
        proxy under SWR (W_{\text{eff}} \le 58.0\text{ kg}) OR achieves a
        wide-lane unchurned outer crown advantage (\text{FTLA}_i \ge 0.45).
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 2.50 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.20.
  - DSP-3 (The Metropolitan Class Gravity Corridor - Class-Drop Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Metropolitan-to-provincial/regional class drop
        active (\text{CMRL}_i = 1) and the runner is prepared by an elite
        metropolitan-grade stable (\Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}} = 0.45).
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 3.00 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.15.
  - DSP-4 (The Fresh-Rail Kinetic Saving - Firm Turf Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Firm, highly elastic turf compaction
        (\text{MSCI}_R \ge 0.80), the runner draws an inside, ground-saving
        starting barrier (B_{\text{actual}} \le 4), and the silo is NOT
        synthetic (\mathbb{I}_{\text{Synthetic\_Active}} = 0).
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 2.00 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.30.

0. Dynamic Micro-Moisture and Compaction Drift (DMM-CD)

Tracks undergo dynamic compaction or liquefaction decay over the course of a
race program. Before calculating any speed or latent frailty metrics,
dynamically calculate the active compaction index (\text{MSCI}_R) for the
current race number R:
\text{MSCI}_R = \text{MSCI}_{\text{initial}} + \delta_{\text{evap}} \cdot (R - 1) - \delta_{\text{precip}} \cdot \sum_{k=1}^{R-1} \text{Rain}_k
Where:

  - \delta_{\text{evap}} = 0.02 if weather is clear/sunny, or 0.00 if
    overcast/wet.
  - \delta_{\text{precip}} = 0.05 per mm of active rainfall recorded prior to
    race R.
  - For wax-bound synthetic tracks, compaction remains structurally static:
    \text{MSCI}_R \leftarrow 0.85. All references to track moisture compaction
    (\text{MSCI}) in subsequent modules must utilise the dynamically drifted
    variable \text{MSCI}_R.

1. Spell-Segmented Form Decay (SSFD) and Surface Decoupling

Calculate the time-weighted Average Beaten Margin
(\text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i}) over the horse's last 3 active starts:
\text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i} = \frac{\sum_{k=1}^{3} \left( w_{k, i} \cdot \text{Margin}_{k, i} \right)}{\sum_{k=1}^{3} w_{k, i}}
Where:
w_{k, i} = e^{-0.005 \cdot \text{Days\_Ago}_{k, i}} \cdot (1.0 - \gamma_{\text{spell}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Spell\_Intervened}, k, i}})
Apply a discount of \gamma_{\text{spell}} = 0.60 if a spell exceeding 120 days
occurred immediately prior to start k.

  - Surface-Form Decoupling Filter (SFDF): If the target race is on Synthetic or
    Good/Dry turf, and the previous runs occurred on Soft/Heavy turf:
    \text{ABM}_{\text{adjusted}, i} = \text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i} \cdot \left(1.0 - \left[ \left(\frac{\text{Wins}_{\text{Dry/Good}, i}}{\max(1, \text{Wins}_{\text{Total}, i})}\right) \cdot \Theta_{\text{dry\_pref}} \right]\right)
    Where \Theta_{\text{dry\_pref}} = 0.80 is the dry preference threshold.
  - Surface-Split Isolation Rule (SSIR): If the runner has \ge 3 synthetic
    starts and exhibits a performance disparity:
    \text{S}_{\text{disp}, i} = \text{Synthetic Place Rate}_i - \text{Turf Place Rate}_i \ge 0.40
    Then strictly calculate \text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i} and \text{FDF}_i
    using only the runner's historical synthetic starts.
  - Class Memory Recovery Lock (\text{CMRL}_i): If a runner has contested
    Group/Listed or Metropolitan Benchmark 84+ company within the last 180 days
    and is dropping to Provincial/Country Benchmark \le 70: Set
    \text{CMRL}_i = 1. If \text{CMRL}_i = 1, cap the Form Decay Factor
    (\text{FDF}_i) to prevent genuine class horses from being mathematically
    written off due to poor raw figures in unsuitable setups:
    \text{FDF}_i = \min( \text{FDF}_i, 0.05 )
  - Form Decay Factor (FDF) Output:
    \text{Adjusted NLS}_i = \text{Raw NLS}_i \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot \max(0, \text{ABM}_{\text{adjusted}, i} - 2.0)}

2. First-Starter and Sire Synthetic/Sand Affinity Modifier (\kappa_{\text{synth}})

\text{Adjusted NLS}_{\text{debut/pedigree}, i} = \text{Adjusted NLS}_i \cdot \kappa_{\text{synth}, i}

  - \kappa_{\text{synth}} = 1.35 for direct progeny of Shamardal, Blue Point,
    Lope de Vega, or Crackerjack King.
  - \kappa_{\text{synth}} = 1.25 for direct progeny of Invincible Spirit,
    Shalaa, or I Am Invincible.
  - \kappa_{\text{synth}} = 1.00 for standard turf-only lines.
  - For unraced debutants on synthetic or sand tracks, add
    \Theta_{\text{synth}} = +5.0 if \kappa_{\text{synth}} \ge 1.25.

3. Course-and-Distance (C&D) Deficit Linear Penalty

If a runner has contested a race at the exact scheduled course and distance
within their last 3 active starts and recorded a beaten margin >2.0\text{L}
(especially where they led and faded), apply a hard, linear deduction directly
to the speed baseline to prevent generic averages from masking cardiorespiratory
decay:
\text{Adjusted NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Adjusted NLS}_i - 1.0 \cdot \text{C\&D\_Beaten\_Margin}_i

4. Closing Sectional Velocity Priority

When calibrating \text{Raw NLS}_i, closing sectional velocity achieved on
comparable rain-affected turf, high-compaction synthetic surfaces, or sand
tracks takes mathematical precedence over generic career-average speed ratings.
Under comparable track configurations, consolidate $\text{Raw NLS}_i using the
runner's highest recent sectional-derived rating.

5. Target Resuming Reset on Synthetic/Sand

If a resuming horse's career synthetic or sand place rate is \ge 50\% over a
minimum of 2 starts, bypass turf-decay functions and reset their
\text{Adjusted NLS}_i to their career-peak synthetic/sand speed figure:
\text{Adjusted NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{NLS}_{\text{synthetic\_peak/sand\_peak}, i}

6. Age-to-Elasticity Speed Degradation & Heavy Equaliser Override

\text{Final NLS}_i = \text{Adjusted NLS}_i - \text{Age\_Decay}_i

  - Standard: \text{Age\_Decay}_i = 2.5 \cdot \max(0, \text{Age}_i - 6)
  - Heavy Equaliser Override: Under wet clay soil bases
    (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.50), high-frequency tendon recoil is less critical than
    absolute muscle-power/aerobic capacity. Wide-drawn veteran runners avoid
    high-suction track areas, mitigating decay. If \text{MSCI}_R \le 0.50 and
    B_{\text{actual}, i} \ge 8, apply a 60% discount to age decay:
    \text{Age\_Decay}_i = 2.5 \cdot \max(0, \text{Age}_i - 6) \cdot 0.40

7. Pace-Induced Cardiovascular Burnout (PICB)

If the Cumulative Field Pace Pressure:
\text{CFPP} = \sum_{j=1}^{N} \text{Lead\_Style\_Metric}_j \cdot e^{-0.15 \cdot B_{\text{actual}, j}}
is \ge 1.80 (highly contested early tempo), apply the Pace Burnout Factor
(\text{PBF}_i) to all runners projected to settle in the leading 30% of the
field: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot \text{PBF}_i
\text{PBF}_i = 1.0 - 0.12 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} - 53.0}{55.0} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{CFPP}}{1.80} \right)

8. Maiden Class-Drop Gravity Alignment (MCGA)

For exposed maiden runners dropping back from highly competitive metropolitan
circuits to country/provincial maiden fields, apply a speed baseline offset to
restore and compensate their true performance capacity under lower pace
densities: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{MCGA}_i
\text{MCGA}_i = 1.50 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Metro\_Maiden\_Drop}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{57.0} \right) \cdot e^{-t_{\text{lay}, i} / 180}
Where \mathbb{I_{\text{Metro\_Maiden\_Drop}, i}} = 1 if dropping from a
metropolitan maiden race or trial to a country/provincial maiden.

9. Viscoelastic Compaction Transition Index (VCTI)

If a track is improving in structural rating on raceday (e.g., Soft 6 to Soft 5)
under high drainage conditions, calculate the transition rate to penalise
heavy-mud specialists who cannot sustain high stride frequencies on compacted
turf: \text{VCTI}_i = \frac{d(\text{MSCI}_R)}{dt}
\text{If } \text{VCTI}_i > 0 \text{ and } \text{MSCI}_{\text{prev\_win}, i} \le 0.50: \quad \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot (1.0 - 0.12 \cdot \text{VCTI}_i)

10. Surface Moisture Elasticity Upgrade (\Delta_{\text{surface\_elasticity}})

When turf moisture decreases and transitions toward a firmer compaction level,
dry-preference runners receive a structural vertical thrust advantage:
\Delta_{\text{surface\_elasticity}, i} = (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Wins}_{\text{Dry/Good}, i}}{\max(1, \text{Wins}_{\text{Wet}, i})} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{57.0} \right)
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Delta_{\text{surface\_elasticity}, i}

11. Aerobic Base Accumulation Index (ABAI)

Provides speed baseline recovery credits for seasoned campaign runners who have
built elite cardiovascular baselines over multiple prep distances, mitigating
poor recent placing records:
\text{ABAI}_i = \ln(N_{\text{runs\_prep}, i}) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Distance}_{\text{prev\_avg}, i}}{1600} \right)^{1.5} \cdot \left( 1.0 + \chi_{\text{stayer}, i} \right)
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{ABAI}_i

12. Distance-Drop Cardiorespiratory Super-Compensation (\Phi_{\text{DD-CSC}})

Models the mitochondrial and vascular expansion advantage of fit stayers
dropping back in distance:
\Phi_{\text{DD-CSC}, i} = 1.85 \cdot \ln(d_{\text{prev}, i} - d_{\text{current}}) \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}
\text{If } (d_{\text{prev}, i} - d_{\text{current}}) \ge 200\text{m and } t_{\text{lay}, i} \le 30\text{d}: \quad \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Phi_{\text{DD-CSC}, i}

13. Respiratory Release Gear Index (\Phi_{\text{resp}})

Restores the speed baseline for horses returning from a noted respiratory
restriction or coughing episode when restrictive headgear is removed:
\Phi_{\text{resp}, i} = 1.25 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Tongue\_Tie\_OFF}, i}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Prev\_Respiratory\_Deficit}, i}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Phi_{\text{resp}, i}

14. Tactical Pathing Resiliency (TPR) Modification to PICB

If a tactical front-runner has the early speed to cross and secure the unchurned
outer crown on a wetting track, discount their Pace-Induced Burnout Factor:
\text{TPR}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{Lead}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{CLB}_i}{\text{PICB}_i} \right) \cdot \Phi_{\text{TLS}, i}
\text{If } \text{TPR}_i > 0.85: \quad \text{PBF}_i \leftarrow 1.0 - 0.40 \cdot (1.0 - \text{PBF}_i)

15. Interstate Stable Gravity Coefficient (C_{\text{gravity}})

Models the class-drop leverage of horses and regional stable transfers from
highly competitive Victorian or NSW metropolitan circuits to provincial/country
South Australian or Queensland environments:
C_{\text{gravity}, i} = 1.75 \cdot \left( \text{Class\_Rating}_{\text{Metro}} - \text{Class\_Rating}_{\text{Current}} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Interstate\_Transfer}, i}} \cdot e^{-t_{\text{lay}, i}/90}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + C_{\text{gravity}, i}

16. Stay-to-Sprint Elasticity Penalty (SSEP)

To prevent overvaluing stayers dropping back severely to sprint assignments
where they lack tactical acceleration:
\text{SSEP}_i = \alpha_{\text{drop}} \cdot \ln\left(\frac{\bar{D}_{\text{prev}, i}}{D_{\text{current}}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{layoff}}} \cdot \left[1.0 + \gamma_{\text{track}} \cdot (\text{MSCI}_R - 0.50)\right]
Where \bar{D}_{\text{prev}, i} is the average distance of its last 3 starts,
\mathbb{I_{\text{layoff}}} = 1.0 if layoff \ge 45\text{ days} (else 0.2),
\alpha_{\text{drop}} = 4.5, and \gamma_{\text{track}} = 1.5.
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i - \text{SSEP}_i

17. Gear Change Focus Modifier (\Gamma_{\text{gear}})

Calculate focus and trajectory improvements from first-time gear modifications:
\Gamma_{\text{gear}, i} = 1.0 + \sum \delta_{\text{gear}, j}

  - \delta_{\text{Blinkers ON}} = +0.035 (active if sprint distance
    \le 1100\text{m} and running style is on-pace/midfield).
  - \delta_{\text{Blinkers OFF}} = +0.015 (active if drawn in
    B_{\text{actual}} \le 3 where relaxation is key).
  - \delta_{\text{Crossover Nose Band (1st Time)}} = +0.04 (improves bit
    alignment).
  - \delta_{\text{Tongue Tie ON (1st Time)}} = +0.04 (prevents respiratory
    displacement under strain).
    \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot \Gamma_{\text{gear}, i}

18. First-Up Neuromuscular Priming Index (FNPI)

Protects explosive first-up performances off layoffs from premature decay
penalties:
\text{If Starts in Prep}_i = 1: \quad \text{FNPI}_i = \max\left(1.0, 1.0 + 0.05 \cdot \ln\left(\frac{t_{\text{lay}, i}}{100}\right) \cdot (1.0 - \text{SDF}_{\text{historical}, i})\right)
And enforce \text{SDF}_i \equiv 0.0.
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot \text{FNPI}_i

19. Let-Up Cardiorespiratory Priming Index (LCPI)

Credits let-ups of 21 to 42 days supported by a sharp barrier trial within 14
days of the race:
\text{LCPI}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{trial}, i}} \cdot 0.15 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot \text{Margin}_{\text{trial}}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{LCPI}_i

20. Class-Elasticity Moisture Interaction Coefficient (\xi_{\text{class-moisture}})

Dynamically elevates class speed figures on dry tracks where class outweighs
weight-drag:
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \left[(\text{MSCI}_R - 0.70) \cdot 1.20 \cdot \text{CPPI}_i\right]

21. Unraced Metro-to-Regional Class Gravity Coefficient (UMR-CGC)

Protects debutants with metropolitan conditioning foundations debuting in
regional country maidens:
\text{UMR-CGC}_i = 2.50 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Stable\_Rating}_{\text{origin}, i}}{\text{Stable\_Rating}_{\text{current}, i}} \right) \cdot e^{-\frac{t_{\text{transfer}, i}}{180}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{UMR-CGC}_i

22. Non-Linear Weight-Relief Advantage (NWRA)

Active in Degenerate Semantic Mode (DSM) to model the physical benefit of
carrying lower weights without explicit weight metrics. Using the horse's
saddlecloth number (N_i) as a continuous weight-relief proxy:
\text{NWRA}_i = \gamma \cdot \ln(N_i) \quad \text{Where } \gamma = 0.80 \text{ under rain-affected conditions } (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.65)
representing higher structural weight relief for higher saddlecloth numbers on
soft turf. Add directly:
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{NWRA}_i

23. Winning Instinct Coefficient (\Psi_{\text{WIC}})

Adds directly to the speed baseline for proven consistent winners dropping back
into regional or provincial handicap classes:
\Psi_{\text{WIC}, i} = 1.50 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Career Wins}_i}{\max(1, \text{Career Starts}_i)} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Class\_Drop\_Active}}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Psi_{\text{WIC}, i}

24. Stable-Track Synergy Modifier (\text{STSM}_i)

Activates on synthetic and country tracks to credit local, surface-specific
conditioning expertise:
\text{STSM}_i = \begin{cases} +2.50 & \text{if Trainer Win Rate on Target Surface/Track } \ge 25\% \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{STSM}_i

25. Blinker & Gear Focus Index (\Phi_{\text{BGFI}})

Captures focus-driven performance shifts on high-kickback synthetic polytracks
or deep sand:
\Phi_{\text{BGFI}, i} = \begin{cases} +1.50 & \text{if Blinkers FIRST TIME, Visors FIRST TIME, Lugging Bit FIRST TIME, or Blinkers OFF} \\ +1.00 & \text{if Blinkers ON (standard application)} \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Phi_{\text{BGFI}, i}

26. Class Merit Floor (\text{CMF}_i) Override

Prevents low-grade lightweights from floating to the top of ratings arrays on
low carried weight alone:
\text{CMF}_i = \begin{cases} -4.00 & \text{if Estimated Class Bench Rating } < 50 \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{CMF}_i

27. Viscoelastic Cohesion Drag Penalty (CD_i)

Quadratically penalises high weights under high wax-binder viscoelastic suction:
CD_i = \alpha_{\text{visc}} \cdot (W_{\text{eff}, i} - 55.0)^2 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Synthetic\_Active}}}
Where \alpha_{\text{visc}} = 0.12 on wax-bound synthetic polytracks,
otherwise 0.00. \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i - CD_i

28. Kinetic Run-Style Congestion Matrix (K-RSCM)

To prevent finishing-order transpositions caused by physical track blockage,
calculate the individual Spatio-Temporal Pocket (\text{STP}_i) and the
comparative Intra-Race Friction (\text{IRF}_{i, j}) relative to preceding
runners: \text{STP}_i = \text{LPI}_i \cdot e^{-0.12 \cdot B_{\text{actual}, i}}
\text{IRF}_{i, j} = \mathbb{I}_{\text{Ahead}(j, i)} \cdot (1.0 - \text{ESI}_j) \cdot e^{-\lambda_{\text{congest}} \cdot |B_{\text{actual}, i} - B_{\text{actual}, j}|}
Where \mathbb{I}_{\text{Ahead}(j, i)} = 1 if runner j settles directly in front
of runner i on the speed map, and \lambda_{\text{congest}} = 0.15. If a
high-ranking horse settles behind a treading or slow runner, apply a direct
speed baseline degradation:
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot \left(1.0 - \alpha_{\text{congest}} \cdot \sum_{j \neq i} \text{IRF}_{i, j}\right)
Where \alpha_{\text{congest}} = 0.05 on turf, and 0.02 on synthetic polytracks.

29. Class-to-Saddlecloth Elasticity Modifier (C-SEM)

Active strictly in Degenerate Semantic Mode (DSM) under turf environments to
prevent bottom-weight "phantom" ratings. If \mathbb{I}_{\text{degenerate}} = 1:
\text{C-SEM}_i = \gamma_{\text{class}} \cdot \ln\left( \frac{N_{\text{max}} - N_i + 1.5}{N_{\text{max}}} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \mathbb{I_{\text{Class\_Memory}, i}})
Where \gamma_{\text{class}} = 1.25. Add directly to Final NLS:
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{C-SEM}_i

30. The Juvenile Straight-Track Professionalism Multiplier (\Phi_{\text{JPM}})

Active strictly on 2YO events over straight courses (Type C Runway). If
N_{\text{starts}, i} = 0 or 1, and the horse has completed \ge 3 official
jumpouts/trials under the preparation of a top-tier local stable (e.g. Hayes,
Maher, Freedman, Waterhouse/Bott):
\Phi_{\text{JPM}, i} = 0.08 \cdot N_{\text{trials}, i} \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot B_{\text{actual}, i}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot (1.0 + \Phi_{\text{JPM}, i})

31. The 2YO Track Hardening Index (\Theta_{\text{TH}})

Under-weights performance configurations of juveniles who achieved career-best
ratings on sweeping provincial layouts when transitioning to high-friction
metropolitan "Bullrings" (e.g. Caulfield, Moonee Valley):
\Theta_{\text{TH}, i} = 2.50 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Transition\_Prov\_to\_Bullring}, i} \cdot \left( 1.0 - e^{-0.05 \cdot N_{\text{starts}, i}} \right)
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i - \Theta_{\text{TH}, i}

32. The Stamina Surplus Overlap (\Phi_{\text{SSO}})

Models the oxygen-conservation advantage of stayers dropping back drastically in
distance (\ge 400\text{m}) into a sprint-middle distance (1300m-1400m) with high
projected pace:
\Phi_{\text{SSO}, i} = 1.75 \cdot \ln\left( d_{\text{prev}, i} - d_{\text{current}} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{CFPP} \ge 1.50} \cdot e^{-0.03 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}
\text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \Phi_{\text{SSO}, i}

Step B: Latent Frailty Calculation (\nu_i)

\nu_i = 0.50 + M_i + F_i - \text{Pedigree\_shield}_i - \Sigma_{\text{syn}, i} - \Phi_{\text{trial\_recovery}, i} - \Lambda_{\text{class}, i} + \Phi_{\text{WTA}, i} + \Delta_{\text{inexp}, i} + \text{PLFI}_i + \Psi_{\text{ADTD}, i} + \Delta_{\text{MMPF}, i} - \Psi_{\text{LSS}, i} - \Phi_{\text{CDSC}, i} + \text{DCDI}_i + \text{SBHI}_i - \Phi_{\text{ABP}, i} + \Delta_{\text{lane}, i} + \Delta_{\text{stress}, i} + T_{\text{fresh\_heavy}, i} - \Phi_{\text{campaign}, i} + \Phi_{\text{MFHD}, i} + \Gamma_{\text{weight\_adjusted}, i} + \Delta_{\text{kickback}, i} + \text{WCVC}_i + \theta_{\text{NGL}, i} - \text{CLB}_i - \Psi_{\text{TIFU}, i} - \Phi_{\text{gear}, i} + \text{KPDF}_i - \Phi_{\text{WBCR}, i} + \text{PDCP}_i + \Phi_{\text{ADPF}, i} + \Delta_{\text{HT-PPRS}, i} + \Delta_{\text{aero}, i} + \Delta_{\text{DRS}, i} + \text{SCD}_i + \text{VSFS}_i - \text{GAFWO}_i + \text{SD-KSI}_i - \text{SKSI}_i - \text{KVSI}_i + \Gamma_{\text{AWT\_torque}, i} - \Phi_{\text{STP}, i} - \text{NGRI}_i + \text{SLR}_i + \Psi_{\text{ASDL}, i} + \text{CRFR}_i - \text{BBS}_i + \text{SCH}_i + \Phi_{\text{LDRB}, i} + \Psi_{\text{crust}, i} - \Phi_{\text{NPR}, i} - \Phi_{\text{NZ-Sink}, i} + \text{TT}_i + \text{LBP}_i + \Gamma_{\text{2YO-Turf}, i} - \Phi_{\text{SSD}, i}

1. The Metabolic Engine (M_i):

M_i = \text{AMDI_{\text{adjusted}, i}} + \text{SDF_{\text{adjusted}, i}} + \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} + \Delta_{\text{shock}, i} + DWP\text{-}WT_i - \text{CPI}_i + \text{NOETF}_i + \text{SURP}_i

  - Asymptotic Metabolic Desynchronisation Index (AMDI):
    \text{AMDI}_i = (1.0 - e^{-0.01 \cdot t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i}}) \cdot \text{MSCI}_R \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \chi_{\text{specialist}, i})
      - Effective Layoff (t_{\text{eff\_lay}}) incorporating Trial Restoration:
        t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i} = t_{\text{lay}, i} \cdot e^{-0.50 \cdot \max(0, N_{\text{trial}, i} - 1)}
        Where N_{\text{trial}, i} is the number of official trials completed
        within 60 days of the run.
      - Aerobic Base Protection (\text{ABP}_i): If a horse has completed \ge 4
        official trials or jumpouts within 60 days of resuming, set
        \text{ABP}_i = 1. This triggers a negative frailty modifier to offset
        structural second-up decay:
        \Phi_{\text{ABP}, i} = 0.30 \cdot \text{ABP}_i
      - Anaerobic Distance Scaling (f_{\text{anaerobic}}): Scale down the
        metabolic penalty for short sprints where anaerobic energy pathways
        dominate:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{scaled}, i} = \text{AMDI}_i \cdot \left[ \frac{1}{1 + e^{-0.01 \cdot (d_{\text{race}} - 1150)}} \right]
      - Home-Track Viscoelastic Habituation Factor (\Theta_{\text{home}}): Apply
        ONLY if the runner is trained on-track at the exact same racecourse as
        the scheduled race. Regional training centres (e.g., Cranbourne,
        Ballarat, Pakenham) do NOT qualify as a home-track advantage for
        distinct metropolitan tracks (e.g., Caulfield, Caulfield Heath,
        Flemington, Moonee Valley). If the training track name and scheduled
        racecourse name do not match identically,
        \Theta_{\text{home}, i} \equiv 0.0. If valid:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{home}, i} = \text{AMDI}_{\text{scaled}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \Theta_{\text{home}}) \quad \text{[where } \Theta_{\text{home}} = 0.75\text{]}
      - Aerodynamic Kickback Shield (\beta_{\text{kickback}}): Confirmed
        front-runners establishing an early rail lead on synthetic or sand
        tracks enjoy a clean-air corridor, reducing metabolic desynchronisation:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} = \text{AMDI}_{\text{home}, i} \cdot ( 1.0 - \delta_{\text{lead}} \cdot \text{GLI}_i ) \quad \text{[where } \delta_{\text{lead}} = 0.35\text{]}
        Where \text{GLI}_i is the Gate Latency Index (derived from trial jump
        efficiency, rated 0.0 to 1.0).
      - Elite-Stable Cardiorespiratory Layoff Discount (\Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}}):
        Models the superior athletic preparation, pre-training facilities, and
        physiological monitoring of elite metropolitan-grade stables, mitigating
        raw cardiorespiratory decay over layoffs in maiden-class fields:
        \Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.45 & \text{if Trainer } \in \{\text{"Ciaron Maher", "Chris Waller", "Ben, Will & JD Hayes", "Anthony & Sam Freedman", "Mick Price & Michael Kent (Jnr)"}\} \text{ and Class } \le \text{Provincial MDN} \\ 1.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot \Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}, i}
      - Genetic Aerobic Hyper-Compensation (\Phi_{\text{GAHC}}): Fully waives
        resuming AMDI penalties for elite first-up performers whose
        cardiovascular output does not decay during layoffs:
        \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i} = \left( \frac{\text{Wins}_{\text{1st-Up}, i}}{\max(1, \text{Starts}_{\text{1st-Up}, i})} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Resuming}, i}} \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot (t_{\text{lay}, i} / 100)}
        \text{If } \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i} \ge 0.50: \quad \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i})
      - Staying Base Exemption (SBE): If
        \text{MaxDistance}_{\text{last 2 starts}} \ge 2400\text{m}, apply a
        mitochondrial preservation discount:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot 0.25
      - Metabolic Trial Mitigation Factor (\gamma_{\text{trial}}): For horses
        off layoffs with recent short-course conditioning:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot e^{-0.35 \cdot N_{\text{trials\_30d}}}
  - Dynamic Cardiorespiratory Flat Risk Formula (\text{CRFR}_i): Replaces static
    second-up syndromic decay with a functional metric of first-up physical
    expenditure. If \text{Starts in Prep}_i = 2:
    \text{CRFR}_i = \left(1.0 - e^{-0.008 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot E_{1\text{U}, i} \cdot e^{-\frac{(t_{\text{backup}, i} - 17)^2}{40.5}}
    Where First-Up Stress Intensity (E_{1\text{U}, i}) is:
    E_{1\text{U}, i} = \text{Pace\_Factor}_{1\text{U}, i} \cdot \ln(1 + \text{beaten\_margin}_{1\text{U}, i}) \cdot \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0}
    With \text{Pace\_Factor}_{1\text{U}} = 1.5 (Led/On-Pace), 1.0 (Midfield),
    or 0.6 (Rear).
      - Second-Up Priming Index (\text{SUPI}): If E_{1\text{U}, i} \le 0.60, or
        if the first-up run occurred on highly unsuitable wet turf
        (\text{MSCI}_{1\text{U}} \le 0.65) and the current target run is firm
        turf/synthetic (\text{MSCI}_R \ge 0.80), deactivate the flat penalty
        (\text{CRFR}_i = 0.0) and apply a recovery credit:
        \text{SUPI}_i = -0.15 \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.05 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0}\right)
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i + \text{SUPI}_i
      - Refined Second-Up Deficit Factor (\text{SDF}_{\text{refined}, i}): If
        E_{1\text{U}, i} > 0.60 and \text{SUPI} conditions are not met, apply:
        \text{SDF}_i = (1.0 - \text{ABP}_i) \cdot 0.35 \cdot e^{-0.08 \cdot (t_{\text{backup}, i} - 12)}
          - Dynamic Age/Progressive Power Modifier (\Gamma_{\text{prog}}):
            Scales down second-up decay for lightly-raced, highly
            progressive 3YOs:
            \Gamma_{\text{prog}, i} = e^{-0.15 \cdot \text{Career Starts}_i} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Age}=3}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Wins}_i}{\text{Career Starts}_i} \right)^{1.5}
          - European Import Hysteresis Exemption (EI-HE): High-class European
            imports are highly resistant to domestic second-up desynchronisation
            and are exempted from the SDF penalty:
            \text{If } \text{Sire\_AWD}_i \ge 1800\text{m and } \text{Origin}_i \in \{\text{FR, GB, IRE}\}: \quad \text{SDF}_i \leftarrow 0.0
          - Adjusted Second-Up Deficit Factor:
            \text{SDF}_{\text{adjusted}, i} = \text{SDF}_i \cdot \left( 1.0 - \Gamma_{\text{prog}, i} \right)
  - Cardiorespiratory Spaced Layoff Recoil (\text{SLR}_i): Penalises detraining
    for horses with excessive spacing between their first and second runs back:
    \text{If } t_{\text{lay}, i} \ge 150\text{ days and } t_{\text{backup}, i} > 45\text{ days}: \quad \text{SLR}_i = +0.15 \cdot \log_{10}(t_{\text{backup}, i} - 40.0)
  - Cardiorespiratory Recovery Decay Index (\text{CRDI}_i): Vetoes horses on a
    quick backup off an uncompetitive first-up defeat:
    \text{CRDI}_i = \left( 1.0 - e^{-\frac{t_{\text{lay}, i}}{28}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Margin}_{t-1, i}}{2.0} \right) \cdot e^{-\frac{t_{\text{backup}, i}}{10}}
    If \text{CRDI}_i > 1.25: Apply Lactic Rebound Deficit Lock (LRDL). Veto 1A
    status and set: \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i \cdot \text{CRDI}_i
  - Metabolic Recoil Modifier (\Delta_{\text{recoil}, i}):
      - Short turnaround peak-performance recoil: If
        t_{\text{lay}} \le 21\text{ days} and \text{CPI}_i = 0.0:
        \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \max(0, R_{t-1, i} - R_{\text{avg}, i}) \cdot \left( \frac{21 - t_{\text{lay}}}{21} \right)
      - Second-up peak-win recoil: \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} = 0.15 if second-up
        off a >120-day spell following a first-up victory, running on a
        \le 21-day backup.
      - Wet-to-Synthetic/Sand Recoil: \Delta_{\text{wet\_recoil}, i} = +0.25 if
        the last start occurred within 14 days on a Heavy 8 or worse turf track.
  - Anaerobic Distance Shock Penalty (\Delta_{\text{shock}, i}): Applied to
    stayers dropping back severely in distance off a long layoff:
    \text{If } \Delta_{\text{dist}} = \text{MaxDistance}_{\text{career}} - \text{Distance}_{\text{current}} \ge 600\text{m and } t_{\text{lay}} \ge 180\text{m}
    \text{Then: } \Lambda_{\text{class}, i} \leftarrow 0.0 \text{ and } \Delta_{\text{shock}, i} = +0.50
  - First-Up Heavy Turf Penalty (T_{\text{fresh\_heavy}, i}): Applied to horses
    resuming off a long layoff (\ge 120 days) on wet, high-viscosity turf tracks
    (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.55), representing the immediate cardiorespiratory
    aerobic tax:
    T_{\text{fresh\_heavy}, i} = 0.15 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Resuming}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right)
  - Campaign-Fitness Advantage (\Phi_{\text{campaign}, i}): Models the superior
    capillary and cardiorespiratory conditioning of active campaign runners
    (\ge 3 active runs) carrying an apprentice's weight allowance under testing
    conditions:
    \Phi_{\text{campaign}, i} = 0.12 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Campaign\_Fit}, i}} \cdot \text{Claim}_{\text{weight}, i}
  - Match-Fitness Hysteresis Discount (\Phi_{\text{MFHD}, i}): Applies an
    aerobic desynchronisation penalty on heavy turf (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.55) to
    debutants or resuming runners when competing against fit campaign runners,
    isolating the gap between trial pace and race pace:
    \Phi_{\text{MFHD}, i} = 0.22 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Resuming/Debutant}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{d_{\text{race}}}{1000} \right)^2 \cdot (1.0 - \text{ABP}_i \cdot 0.50)
  - Dynamic Wetting-Phase Weight Tax (DWP\text{-}WT_i): Calculates the
    non-linear metabolic surcharge of carrying high dead weight on clay-heavy
    soil bases during active wetting phases under rain-affected conditions
    (\text{Viscoelastic Hysteresis} = \text{"Wetting"}):
    DWP\text{-}WT_i = \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot \text{MSCI}_R \cdot (W_{\text{eff}, i} - 55.0)^{1.5} \cdot (1.0 + \mu_{\text{wetting}}) \cdot \Gamma_{\text{PCR}, i}
    Where \mu_{\text{wetting}} = 0.25 if active rain is falling, otherwise 0.00.
    If Hysteresis is Static or Drying, DWP\text{-}WT_i \equiv 0.00.
  - Cardiovascular Priming Index (\text{CPI}_i): Models the physiological
    super-compensation of a short-interval backup off a highly taxing wet-track
    run, converting short-backup recoil penalties into metabolic recovery
    credits:
    \text{CPI}_i = 0.20 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Backup} \le 7}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_{\text{prev}}) \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot \text{Margin}_{\text{prev}}} \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{campaign}, i})
  - Neurological Over-Excitation Tension Factor (NOETF): Captures the metabolic
    depletion when highly-strung young horses pull or over-race early under high
    pace pressure:
    \text{NOETF}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{Gear\_Change}, i}} \cdot e^{-\lambda_{\text{experience}} \cdot N_{\text{starts}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{CFPP}}{1.50} \right) \cdot \Psi_{\text{excitation}, i}
    Where \Psi_{\text{excitation}} = 0.35 represents highly-strung sire lines.
  - Surface-Transition Priming Coefficient (\Phi_{\text{STP}}): Models the
    cardiorespiratory stimulus of backing up within 7 days off a high-viscosity,
    rain-affected turf run to dry sand:
    \Phi_{\text{STP}, i} = \ln\left( \frac{\text{Viscosity}_{\text{prior\_turf}}}{\text{Viscosity}_{\text{current\_dirt}}} \right) \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot t_{\text{lay}}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Transition}}}
    \text{If } \Phi_{\text{STP}, i} \ge 0.40: \quad M_i \leftarrow M_i - 0.45 \cdot \Phi_{\text{STP}, i}
  - Anaerobic Sharpness Deficit off Layoffs (\Psi_{\text{ASDL}}): Quantifies
    backmarker deceleration in a crawling sit-and-sprint race off a spell:
    \Psi_{\text{ASDL}, i} = \theta_{\text{tempo}} \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.02 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot \chi_{\text{slow-begin}, i}
    Where \theta_{\text{tempo}} = 1.5 if predicted tempo is slow
    (\text{CFPP} \le 1.0), and 0.2 if fast. \chi_{\text{slow-begin}, i} is the
    percentage of last 5 starts where the horse was slow to begin.
  - Second-Up Recoil Penalty (\text{SURP}_i): Dynamic frailty correction mapping
    structural spell-recoil back into the metabolic engine rather than binary
    sifting. If V_{\text{SR}, i} = 1:
    \text{SURP}_i = +0.65 \text{ (Added directly to Metabolic Engine } M_i\text{)}
  - Thermal Tax / Heat Stress Modifier (\text{TT}_i): Models the physiological
    toll of extreme temperatures (T_{\text{ambient}} \ge 32^\circ\text{C}):
    \text{TT}_i = \alpha_{\text{thermal}} \cdot \max(0, T_{\text{ambient}} - 32.0) \cdot W_{\text{eff}, i} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{field\_density}}
    Where \alpha_{\text{thermal}} = 0.015, and
    \mathbb{I}_{\text{field\_density}} = 1.0 if N_{\text{active}} \ge 8,
    and 0.60 if N_{\text{active}} < 8 (reducing the tax under minimal tactical
    pressure).

2. The Mechanical Engine (F_i):

F_i = f_{\text{poly\_final}, i} + D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} + \Delta_{\text{decouple}, i} + \Delta_{\text{age}, i} + \text{SEDI}_i + \Psi_{\text{ortho}, i} + \Gamma_{\text{traffic}, i} + \Psi_{\text{chute}, i} + \text{TWAD}_i + \text{CBTS}_i + \text{VSPCI}_i + \Delta f_{\text{plate}, i} + \text{ASST}_i

  - Polytrack Viscoelastic Friction (f_{\text{poly}, i}):
    f_{\text{poly}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{60.0} \right)^2 \cdot ( 1.0 + 0.05 \cdot \max(0, T_{\text{temp}} - 20) ) \cdot d_{\text{pen}, i}
    Where Viscoelastic Penetration Depth (d_{\text{pen}, i}) is:
    d_{\text{pen}, i} = \left[ \frac{m_i \cdot g \cdot \cos(\theta)}{\sigma_{\text{crust}} \cdot A_{\text{hoof}}} \right] \cdot ( 1 + \beta_{\text{temp}} \cdot \max(0, T_{\text{track}} - 15) ) \cdot \left( \frac{\mu_{\text{visc}}}{\omega_{\text{s}, i}} \right)
      - Dynamic Traction Modifier under Wet Synthetic Wax (\mu_{\text{visco}}):
        If moisture is high (H > 80\%), wet-lubrication constant
        (k_{\text{moist}} = 0.15) and sire AWD adjust traction:
        \mu_{\text{visco}, i} = \mu_0 \cdot e^{-k_{\text{moist}} \cdot H} \cdot [ 1 - \gamma_{\text{slip}} \cdot ( \text{AWD}_i / 1000 ) ]
      - Sandy-Loam & Clay-Loam Turf Friction Modifier (\beta_{\text{soil}}): For
        turf races, scale the surface friction penalty by soil type, drainage
        profile, and vertical suction:
        F_i \leftarrow F_i \cdot \beta_{\text{soil}}
        \beta_{\text{soil}} = \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot \mu_{\text{moisture}} \cdot (1.0 - 0.75 \cdot \Phi_{\text{StrathAyr}}) \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{suction}})
        Where \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} is specified in the track database.
          - Sandy-Loam Turf Decoupling Modifier: Sandy-loam turf with \le 35\%
            clay is highly porous, causing faster moisture dissipation. If soil
            type is Sandy-Loam and clay content \le 0.35, reduce cohesive clay
            suction penalties by 45%:
            \beta_{\text{soil}} \leftarrow \beta_{\text{soil}} \cdot 0.55
      - Wax Temperature Modifier (\theta_{\text{wax}}): Scales polymer drag
        based on track temperature:
        \theta_{\text{wax}} = \max\left(0.50, \min\left(1.20, 0.50 + 0.05 \cdot (T_{\text{track}} - 10.0)\right)\right)
        f_{\text{poly\_final}, i} = f_{\text{poly}, i} \cdot \theta_{\text{wax}}
      - Viscoelastic Slipstream Pre-Compaction Index (VSPCI): Models mechanical
        efficiency behind front-runners on cold, cohesive tracks
        (T_{\text{track}} < 15^\circ\text{C}):
        \text{VSPCI}_i = \begin{cases} 1.0 & \text{for } \text{ESI}_i \ge 0.80 \\ 1.0 - \theta_{\text{temp}} \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.1 \cdot N_{\text{lead}}}\right) & \text{for } \text{ESI}_i < 0.80 \end{cases}
        Where
        \theta_{\text{temp}} = \max\left(0, \frac{15.0 - T_{\text{track}}}{15.0}\right).
        Apply directly to f_{\text{poly\_final}, i}.
      - Concussion Plate Friction Modifier (\Delta f_{\text{plate}}): Active
        only if Gear == "Concussion Plates (Front) ON" on synthetic track
        profiles:
        \Delta f_{\text{plate}, i} = 0.08 \cdot \left(\frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{60.0}\right) \cdot \mu_{\text{visc}}
  - Geometric Turn Drag (D_{\text{geometry}, i}):
    D_{\text{geometry}, i} = 0.25 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} \cdot B_{\text{effective}, i}}{\text{Circumference}} \right) \cdot \theta_{\text{run-up}} \cdot \lambda_{\text{settle}} \cdot \left( \frac{250}{d_{\text{turn}}} \right) \cdot (1.0 + \text{LLDD}_i)
      - Tempo-Specific Settling Coefficient (\kappa_{\text{settle}}): Adjusts
        mechanical drag relative to pace dynamics:
        \kappa_{\text{settle}, i} = 1.0 + \sigma_{\text{tempo}} \cdot \left( \text{Settle\_Pattern}_i - \text{Ideal\_Settle} \right)
        For slow tempos (\text{CFPP} \le 1.0): \text{Ideal\_Settle} = 1.5 and
        \sigma_{\text{tempo}} = 0.5.
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} = D_{\text{geometry}, i} \cdot \kappa_{\text{settle}, i}
      - Early-Pace Turn Drag Mitigation (EPTDM):
        B_{\text{effective}, i} = \max(1, B_{\text{actual}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \text{ESI}_i^2))
        Where \text{ESI}_i \in [0, 1] is the horse's historical frequency of
        leading.
      - Dynamic Gate Speed Discount (\delta_{\text{gate}}): If the run-up to the
        turn is \ge 250\text{m}, discount the geometric penalty for
        front-runners who cross the field before the turn:
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \leftarrow D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \cdot e^{-0.15 \cdot ( d_{\text{turn}} / 100 )}
      - Draft-Induced Drag Mitigation (DIDM): Reduces geometric drag for horses
        mapped to draft behind speed-setters:
        \text{DIDM}_i = 0.12 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Draft\_Pocket}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{CFPP}}{1.50} \right)
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \leftarrow D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \text{DIDM}_i)
      - Lateral Lane Displacement Drag (LLDD): Scales turn drag in short sprints
        (<1100\text{m}) based on lateral lane pathing around the turn:
        \text{LLDD}_i = \left( \frac{\text{Lane Number} - 1}{\text{Track Radius}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Race Distance}}{1000} \right)
      - Starting runway decay modifier (\theta_{\text{run-up}}):
        \theta_{\text{run-up}} = 1.0 \text{ if } d_{\text{run-up}} < 250\text{m, else } e^{-0.005 \cdot (d_{\text{run-up}} - 250)}
      - Run-style settle discount (\lambda_{\text{settle}}):
        \lambda_{\text{settle}} = 0.50 \text{ if } B_{\text{actual}, i} \ge 8 \text{ and horse settles in the rear 30\% of the field, else } 1.00
      - Fresh Turf Lane Advantage (\text{FTLA}_i): Applied strictly during the
        wetting phase on clay-heavy tracks where compacted inner paths exhibit
        high suction. If \text{Hysteresis} = \text{"Wetting"} and
        B_{\text{actual}, i} \ge 8, apply a discount to the geometric drag to
        reflect the benefit of navigating unchurned outer turf lanes:
        \text{FTLA}_i = 0.40 \cdot \text{ABP}_i + 0.15 \cdot \text{Lane\_Preference\_Score}_i
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \leftarrow D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \text{FTLA}_i)
      - Jockey Tactical Navigation Index (\text{JTNI}): Models elite riders
        seeking the least-degraded fresh outer paths wide on the home turn
        during rain-affected conditions. Turn drag is discounted to represent
        this tactical search pattern:
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \leftarrow D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{TLS}, i})
        \Phi_{\text{TLS}, i} = 0.40 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Elite\_Jockey}, i}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot S_{\text{visc}} \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot (B_{\text{actual}, i} - 1)}
        Where \mathbb{I_{\text{Elite\_Jockey}, i}} = 1 if an elite
        metropolitan-grade jockey is booked to ride, and S_{\text{visc}} is the
        clay suction factor.
      - Small Field Scaling Override: If N_{\text{active}} \le 8, scale down
        geometric turn drag:
        D_{\text{geometry\_adjusted}, i} = D_{\text{geometry}, i} \cdot \left( \frac{N_{\text{active}}}{12} \right) \cdot [1.0 + \sigma_{\text{tempo}} \cdot ( \text{Settle\_Pattern}_i - \text{Ideal\_Settle} )]
  - Trapped-Wide Aerodynamic Wind Drag (TWAD): Penalises horses forced to cover
    extra ground without cover on sweeping turns:
    \text{TWAD}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{No\_Cover}, i}} \cdot \rho_{\text{air}} \cdot v_{\text{wind}} \cdot \cos(\theta_{\text{wind}}) \cdot \left( \frac{B_{\text{actual}, i}}{\text{Radius}_{\text{turn}}} \right)^2 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right)
    Where \mathbb{I_{\text{No\_Cover}}} = 1 if wider than two-wide without a
    drafting partner.
  - Clockwise Biomechanical Transition Surcharge (CBTS): Captures
    turn-navigation stress for interstate runners transitioning off a long
    layoff:
    \text{CBTS}_i = 0.15 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Direction\_Change}, i}} \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.01 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right)
  - Sprint-Chute Turn Drag Penalty (\Psi_{\text{chute}, i}): Applied to
    wide-drawn runners over short sprint distances (\le 1100\text{m}) where the
    run-up to the turn is <250\text{m}, forcing them to immediately negotiate
    the bend wide under maximum anaerobic output:
    \Psi_{\text{chute}, i} = 0.35 \cdot \text{MSCI}_R^{-1} \cdot \left( \frac{B_{\text{actual}, i} - 1}{\text{Track Radius}} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Short\_Sprint}, i}}
  - Traffic Density Penalty (\Gamma_{\text{traffic}, i}): Applied to wide-drawn
    runners when forced to compete against multiple interior front-runners:
    \Gamma_{\text{traffic}, i} = \max\left(0, \ln(N_{\text{speed\_competitors}}) \cdot \left( \frac{B_{\text{actual}, i} - 4}{10} \right) \cdot \text{MSCI}_R\right)
    Where N_{\text{speed\_competitors}} is the number of runners inside barrier
    B_{\text{actual}, i} with an average first-sectional beaten margin
    <1.5\text{L}.
  - Spatial Blockage Hazard Index (\text{SBHI}_i): Models the propensity of
    inside-drawn backmarkers to become trapped behind tiring front-runners along
    the rails:
    \text{SBHI}_i = 0.25 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{B_{\text{actual}, i} \le 3} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Settle\_Rear\_30pc}}} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{CFPP}}{1.50} \right) \cdot \ln(N_{\text{runners}})
  - Viscoelastic Wet-Track Sectional Decoupling (\Delta_{\text{decouple}, i}):
      - On turf (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.70): \Delta_{\text{decouple}, i} = +0.40 if
        Soft-Surface Stride Efficiency (SSSE) is <0.95.
      - On synthetic: \Delta_{\text{decouple}, i} = +0.40 for wet-turf
        specialists (wet-track win rate \ge 40\%) making their synthetic debut.
  - Age-to-Elasticity Decay (\Delta_{\text{age}, i}): Captures tendon recoil
    loss in older horses on high-compaction surfaces:
    \Delta_{\text{age}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \max(0, \text{Age}_i - 6) \cdot \text{MSCI}_R
  - Synthetic Experience Deficit Index (\text{SEDI}_i): Progressively penalises
    horses lacking exposure to synthetic kickback and specific rebound profiles:
    \text{SEDI}_i = 0.45 \cdot e^{-0.60 \cdot N_{\text{synthetic\_starts}, i}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Synthetic\_Active}}}
  - Orthopaedic Efficiency Modifier (\Psi_{\text{ortho}, i}):
    \Psi_{\text{ortho}, i} = -0.15 \text{ if utilising Synthetic Hoof Filler for the first time on synthetic.}
  - Wide-Cover Vulnerability Coefficient (WCVC_i): Calculates the mechanical
    turn-drag penalty of being trapped wide without cover on turns:
    P_{\text{wide}, i} = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-0.28 \cdot (B_{\text{actual}, i} - 6.0) \cdot (1.0 - \text{ESI}_i)}}
    \text{If } P_{\text{wide}, i} \ge 0.65: \quad WCVC_i = 0.15 \cdot P_{\text{wide}, i} \cdot \beta_{\text{soil}}
    \text{If } P_{\text{wide}, i} < 0.65: \quad WCVC_i \equiv 0.00
  - Neurological Gate Lag Coefficient (\theta_{\text{NGL}, i}): Calculates the
    neurological risk of starting-gate delay. This penalty is heavily amplified
    for second-up runners coming off highly demanding first-up prep runs:
    \theta_{\text{NGL}, i} = 0.22 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Second-Up}, i}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{ABP}_i) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Margin\_first\_up} \le 1.5\text{L}}} \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot (t_{\text{lay}} - 14)}
    Where \mathbb{I_{\text{Margin\_first\_up} \le 1.5\text{L}}} identifies a
    highly competitive first-up run within 1.5 lengths.
  - Viscoelastic Sinking Friction Surcharge (\text{VSFS}): Measures energy loss
    in loose, high-friction sand:
    \text{VSFS}_i = \gamma_{\text{sand}} \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} - 55.0}{55.0} \right)^2 \cdot \left( 1.0 + \beta_{\text{wide}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{No\_Cover}, i}} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \Theta_{\text{sand}, i})
    Where \gamma_{\text{sand}} = 0.35 and \beta_{\text{wide}} = 0.25.
  - Geometrically Adjusted Friction-Work Offset (\text{GAFWO}): Models kinetic
    energy conservation of rails-hugging pathing under sand track drag:
    \text{GAFWO}_i = \mu_{\text{sand}} \cdot g \cdot (M_{\text{horse}} + W_{\text{eff}, i}) \cdot \left[ \Delta D_{\text{rail}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \text{LLDD}_i) \right]
  - Loose Sand Kickback Saturation Index (\text{SD-KSI}): Models airway friction
    and sensory distress when trailing mid-field:
    \text{SD-KSI}_i = \alpha_{\text{sand}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{LPI}_i) \cdot \ln(N_{\text{runners\_ahead}} + 1.5) \cdot e^{-\beta_{\text{cover}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{draft}}}}
    Where \alpha_{\text{sand}} = 0.45.
  - Sensory Kickback Shield Index (\text{SKSI}): Models the mitigation of
    particulate sensory shock via headgear applications:
    \text{SKSI}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{Blinkers\_On}, i}} \cdot \alpha_{\text{kickback}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{LPI}_i)
    Where \alpha_{\text{kickback}} = 0.85.
  - Kickback Vortex Suppression Index (\text{KVSI}): Represents early mechanical
    crossing to secure clean air:
    \text{KVSI}_i = \text{LPI}_i \cdot \left( 1.0 - \frac{\text{Position}_i - 1}{N_{\text{active}}} \right)^{1.5} \cdot e^{-\lambda_{\text{sand}} \cdot \text{MSCI}_R}
    Where \lambda_{\text{sand}} = 0.35.
  - Apprentice Synthetic Strength Tax (\text{ASST}_i): Models physical fatigue
    on high-viscosity synthetic straight tracks:
    \text{ASST}_i = \mathbb{I_{\text{Apprentice}}} \cdot \alpha_{\text{drag}} \cdot \text{ACH}_i \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Synthetic\_Active}}}
    Where \alpha_{\text{drag}} = 0.50 on synthetic wax-bound tracks,
    otherwise 0.00.
  - Straight-Track Lane Bias Penalty (LBP_i): Active strictly on straight runway
    layouts (e.g. Flemington Straight) when the rail is in the True position and
    \text{MSCI}_R \ge 0.70:
    \text{LBP}_i = \begin{cases} +0.15 & \text{if } B_{\text{actual}, i} \le 3 \text{ (Inside lane degradation)} \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}

3. Biological, Class, and Weight Modifiers:

  - Pedigree Shield Decay (PSD): Scale down the pedigree shield for out-of-form
    stayer:
    \text{Pedigree\_shield}_i \leftarrow \text{Pedigree\_shield}_i \cdot e^{-0.25 \cdot \max(0, \text{ABM}_{\text{adjusted}, i} - 2.0)}
  - Maternal Wet-Stamina Pedigree Shield Override: If the runner's sire AWD is
    \le 1200\text{ m} (representing a pure speed influence), but their maternal
    grandsire wet rating is \ge 8.5 (e.g. Tavistock), apply a +0.15 structural
    pedigree shield modifier to neutralise metabolic lactic build-up over
    distances \ge 1400\text{ m} on turf where \text{MSCI}_R \le 0.50:
    \text{Pedigree\_shield}_i \leftarrow \text{Pedigree\_shield}_i + 0.15
  - Synthetic Surface Affinity Factor (\Sigma_{\text{syn}, i}): Quantifies past
    dynamic habituation and visual/respiratory comfort under synthetic kickback:
    \Sigma_{\text{syn}, i} = 0.40 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Synthetic Wins}_i}{\max(1, \text{Synthetic Starts}_i)} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Synthetic Starts}_i}{\text{Career Starts}_i} + 0.50 \right) + \text{Habituation\_Credit}_i
    Where \text{Habituation\_Credit}_i = 0.20 if the runner placed 2nd or 3rd at
    their only prior synthetic start, otherwise 0.00.
  - Trial-Induced Metabolic Restoration Offset
    (\Phi_{\text{trial\_recovery}, i}):
    \Phi_{\text{trial\_recovery}, i} = 0.20 \text{ if } N_{\text{trial}} \ge 3 \text{ trials within 60 days of resuming.}
  - Class Drop Elasticity Offset (\Lambda_{\text{class}, i}): Reflects the
    advantage of dropping class onto a stable, consistent surface, particularly
    on regional turf/synthetic/sand circuits. To prevent dynamic frailty
    deflation, class-drop offsets are isolated and capped at a maximum combined
    credit of -0.15 to the latent frailty (\nu_i):
    \Lambda_{\text{class}, i} = \min\left(0.15, \ln\left( \frac{\text{Class}_{\text{prior}, i}}{\text{Class}_{\text{current}, i}} \right) \cdot \beta_{\text{track}} \cdot (1.0 + \text{T\&D\_spec}_i)\right)
    Where \beta_{\text{track}} = 1.5 for regional circuits and 1.0 for
    metropolitan tracks, and \text{T\&D\_spec} is the track-and-distance rating
    (0.0 to 0.50).
  - Class-Drop Performance Offset (C_{\text{offset}, i}): Introduces a speed
    restoration offset for metropolitan-grade horses dropping back to
    provincial/country benchmark fields:
    \text{Final NLS}_i = \text{Final NLS}_i + C_{\text{offset}, i}
    C_{\text{offset}, i} = 1.25 \cdot ( \text{Metro\_Grade}_{\text{highest}, i} - \text{Prov\_Grade}_{\text{current}, i} ) \cdot e^{-t_{\text{lay}, i} / 365}
  - Weight-to-Age and Sex Vulnerability (\Phi_{\text{WTA}, i}): Penalises
    immature female gallopers carrying high weights against older male rivals:
    \Phi_{\text{WTA}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \text{Is3YO}_i \cdot \text{IsFilly}_i \cdot \max(0, W_{\text{eff}, i} - 55.0) \cdot \Gamma_{\text{PCR}, i}
  - Metabolic Inexperience Penalty (\Delta_{\text{inexp}, i}): Applied to
    debutants in open-age maiden events against seasoned placing opponents:
    \Delta_{\text{inexp}, i} = 0.15 \cdot ( 1.0 - \text{Age}_i / 4.0 ) \quad \text{(Age capped at 4.0 years for debutants in open-age maidens)}
  - Prep Longevity Fatigue Index (\text{PLFI}_i): Calculates accumulated
    campaign fatigue, scaling exponentially under rain-affected conditions
    (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.70):
    \text{PLFI}_i = 0.05 \cdot \max(0, N_{\text{prep}, i} - 4) \cdot \left(1.0 + \frac{\text{N}_{\text{career}, i}}{50}\right) \cdot (1.0 + 1.5 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R))
  - Aerobic Distance Transition Deficit (\Psi_{\text{ADTD}, i}):
    \Psi_{\text{ADTD}, i} = \max(0, d_{\text{race}} - d_{\text{max\_win}, i}) \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{60.0} \right) \cdot ( 1.0 - \chi_{\text{stayer}, i} ) \cdot \text{MSCI}_R
  - Maternal Metabolic Peak Fatigue (\Delta_{\text{MMPF}, i}):
    \Delta_{\text{MMPF}, i} = 0.25 \cdot \text{IsMare}_i \cdot \max(0, \text{Age}_i - 5) \cdot \left( \frac{10 - t_{\text{lay}}}{10} \right) \cdot \text{Wins}_{\text{last3}, i}
  - Cardiorespiratory Distance-Drop Super-Compensation (\Phi_{\text{CDSC}, i}):
    Applied as a negative modifier to Latent Frailty (\nu_i):
    \Phi_{\text{CDSC}, i} = 0.35 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Distance}_{\text{prev}, i} - \text{Distance}_{\text{current}}}{200} \right) \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}
  - Distance Compression Deficit Index (\text{DCDI}_i): Applied as a positive
    modifier to Latent Frailty (\nu_i):
    \text{DCDI}_i = 0.0005 \cdot \max(0, \text{Distance}_{\text{prev\_avg}, i} - \text{Distance}_{\text{current}, i}) \cdot e^{-0.05 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}
  - Latent Stable Selection Coefficient (\Psi_{\text{LSS}, i}): Derived from
    stable-jockey-track synergy metrics:
      - Elite Metropolitan Jockey booked for a premier stable:
        \Psi_{\text{LSS}, i} \in [-0.22, -0.25] (e.g. Craig Williams riding for
        Lindsay Park).
      - Provincial-Grade Jockey booked for an out-of-region stable:
        \Psi_{\text{LSS}, i} \in [-0.10, -0.12].
      - Formula:
        \Psi_{\text{LSS}, i} = \text{SR}_{\text{Jockey\_Stable}, i} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{SR}_{\text{Stable\_Track}, i}}{\text{Mean\_SR}_{\text{Jockey\_All\_Stable}}} \right) \cdot (1.0 - e^{-0.10 \cdot N_{\text{starts\_partnership}}})
  - Degraded Lane Penalty / Gutter Suction Trap Modifier
    (\Delta_{\text{lane}, i}): Models the metabolic and mechanical drag of
    racing in degraded lanes when settling off the pace:
    \Delta_{\text{lane}, i} = 0.25 \cdot (1.0 - \text{LPI}_i) S_{\text{visc}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{Lane\_Preference\_Score}_i)
    Where \text{LPI}_i \in [0.0, 1.0] represents the horse's projected early
    speed position, and the Viscoelastic Suction Coefficient (S_{\text{visc}})
    is:
    S_{\text{visc}} = (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{StrathAyr}}) \cdot \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot e^{1.5 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R)}
    This penalty isolates and applies strictly when a horse is forced to settle
    off the pace on wet, high-suction clay tracks. For sand-based StrathAyr
    systems (\Phi_{\text{StrathAyr}} = 1), S_{\text{visc}} is zeroed, and
    \Delta_{\text{lane}, i} \equiv 0.0.
  - Debutant Metabolic Stress Penalty (\Delta_{\text{stress}, i}): Applied to
    unraced debutants (N_{\text{starts}, i} = 0) to model the cardiorespiratory
    shock of their first career start under full racing pressure:
    \Delta_{\text{stress}, i} = \mathbb{I}_{N_{\text{starts}}=0} \cdot 0.20 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \left( \frac{d_{\text{race}}}{1000} \right)
    Where d_{\text{race}} is the race distance and \text{MSCI}_R is the track
    compaction level. This adds directly to latent frailty, scaling up on wet,
    deep tracks.
  - Dynamic Weight-Elasticity Thresholds (DWET): Calculates the consolidated,
    non-linear vertical shear sinkage tax of carrying heavy dead weight over
    rain-affected turf. This equation integrates the clay-content suction
    penalty directly into the weight tax structure, replacing linear weight
    formulas:
    \Gamma_{\text{weight}, i} = [0.18 + (0.32 \cdot \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{StrathAyr}}))] \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \max(0, W_{\text{eff}, i} - 55.0)^{\gamma_{\text{elastic}}} \cdot (1.0 - 0.50 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Claim}, i}}) \cdot (1.0 - \Psi_{\text{MCC}, i})
    Where the dynamic elasticity exponent \gamma_{\text{elastic}} scales
    non-linearly over longer staying trips under low compaction:
    \gamma_{\text{elastic}} = 1.0 + \left( \frac{d_{\text{race}}}{1200} \right)^{1.5} \cdot \left( 1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R \right)^2
    Apply the Viscoelastic-to-Elastic Compaction Hysteresis
    (\Gamma_{\text{VE}\to\text{E}}) to scale down the final weight tax as the
    track transitions towards dry firm turf:
    \Gamma_{\text{weight\_adjusted}, i} = \Gamma_{\text{weight}, i} \cdot \left(1.0 - \tanh\left[5.0 \cdot \left(\text{MSCI}_R - 0.70\right)\right]\right)
  - Kickback Drag Modifier (\Delta_{\text{kickback}, i}): Calculates the
    metabolic and mechanical drag of racing behind other horses on wet turf,
    leading to involuntary stride shortening:
    \Delta_{\text{kickback}, i} = 0.30 \cdot (1.0 - \text{LPI}_i) \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot S_{\text{visc}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{CAC}_i)
    Where \text{CAC}_i = 1 (Clean-Air Corridor) if the horse is leading
    (\text{LPI}_i \ge 0.80) or navigating the wide outer lanes
    (\Phi_{\text{TLS}, i} \ge 0.15), otherwise 0.0.
  - Compaction Lane Bias (\text{CLB}_i): Models the physiological and mechanical
    advantage of tracking wide on the drier, unchurned outer crown of a
    rain-affected clay track under active wetting conditions:
    \text{CLB}_i = 0.22 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Hysteresis}=\text{"Wetting"}}} \cdot \max(0, B_{\text{actual}, i} - 5)
  - Mass-to-Class Compensatory Ratio (\Psi_{\text{MCC}, i}): Prevents the
    over-penalisation of high-class performers carrying topweight against
    low-grade country benchmark fields:
    \Psi_{\text{MCC}, i} = \min\left(0.35, 0.12 \cdot (\text{Class}_{\text{prior}, i} - \text{Class}_{\text{current}, i}) \cdot e^{-\beta_{\text{soil}} \cdot \text{MSCI}_R}\right)
  - Progressive Class Reserve Modifier (\Gamma_{\text{PCR}, i}): Reduces the
    dynamic weight and age taxes for highly progressive, unexposed young
    prospects carrying weight on soft turf surfaces:
    \Gamma_{\text{PCR}, i} = \max\left(0.20, e^{-0.15 \cdot (\text{Margin}_{\text{debut}} - 3.0)}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Starts} \le 2}}
  - Soil-Base Suction Partition (\Phi_{\text{suction}, i}): Automatically
    deactivates structural cohesive clay suction penalties on highly porous
    sand-loam track bases (\alpha_{\text{clay}} \le 0.20):
    \Phi_{\text{suction}, i} = \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{StrathAyr}})
  - Debutant Freshness Stamina Allocation (\Phi_{\text{fresh}, i}): Credits
    unexposed first-starters on heavy turf surfaces with maternal stamina
    reserves to balance physical startup shock:
    \Phi_{\text{fresh}, i} = \mathbb{I}_{N_{\text{starts}}=0} \cdot 0.15 \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{AWD}_{\text{Sire}, i}}{1200} \right)
  - Trainer Intent First-Up Highway Index (\Psi_{\text{TIFU}, i}): Dynamic
    frailty credit to prevent the over-penalisation of elite regional stables
    when resuming first-up in metropolitan Highway Class races:
    \Psi_{\text{TIFU}, i} = \mathbb{I_{\text{Highway}}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Resuming} \ge 60\text{d}}} \cdot \text{Claim}_{\text{apprentice}} \cdot \Theta_{\text{Trainer\_Highway\_SR}}
    Where \Theta_{\text{Trainer\_Highway\_SR}} \ge 0.18 represents targeted
    master conditioners.
  - Gear Change Aerobic Stimulus (\Phi_{\text{gear}, i}): Models the linear
    force vectoring and lateral head sway reduction when blinkers/visors are
    applied first-time or re-applied:
    \Phi_{\text{gear}, i} = 1.25 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Blinkers\_1st\_Time}, i}} \cdot \left( \frac{1100}{d_{\text{race}}} \right)
  - Kickback Traction Dissipation Factor (KPDF): Adjusts the sensory distress
    and mechanical friction penalty when forced to trail inside paths under
    rain-affected conditions:
    \text{KPDF}_i = (1.0 - \mathbb{I_{\text{CAA}, i}}) \cdot \alpha_{\text{clay\_content}} \cdot \text{MSCI}_R^{-1} \cdot (1.0 - \text{LPI}_i) \cdot \Delta_{\text{kickback}, i}
  - Waterhouse/Bott Cardio Resilience Index (\Phi_{\text{WBCR}, i}):
    \Phi_{\text{WBCR}, i} = 0.25 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Waterhouse/Bott}, i}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \Phi_{\text{campaign}, i}
  - Pedigree-to-Class Drop Compatibility Penalty (PDCP):
    \text{PDCP}_i = 0.35 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Class\_Memory}, i}} \cdot (1.0 - \mathbb{I_{\text{Sire\_Stamina\_Match}, i}})
  - Apprentice-to-Distance Pace Recovery Factor (\Phi_{\text{ADPF}, i}):
    \Phi_{\text{ADPF}, i} = 0.20 \cdot \text{ACH}_i \cdot \left( \frac{1200}{d_{\text{race}}} \right)
  - Heavy-Track Place-to-Prep Recoil Score (\Delta_{\text{HT-PPRS}, i}):
    \Delta_{\text{HT-PPRS}, i} = 0.40 \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Starts in Prep} \ge 3, i}} \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Last\_Run\_Heavy}, i}} \cdot (W_{\text{eff}, i} - 54.0)
  - Apprentice Aerobic Energy Recovery Offset (\Delta_{\text{aero}, i}):
    \Delta_{\text{aero}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \text{ACH}_i \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Campaign\_Fit}, i}}
  - Distance Run-up Surcharge (\Delta_{\text{DRS}, i}):
    \Delta_{\text{DRS}, i} = 0.35 \cdot \max(0, d_{\text{race}} - 1400)
  - Saddlecloth Class Decay Index (\text{SCD}_i): Active ONLY in Degenerate
    Semantic Mode (DSM) to penalise bottom-weight, low-bench runners lacking
    class indicators:
    \text{SCD}_i = 0.85 \cdot \left( \frac{N_i - 1}{N_{\text{max}} - 1} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \mathbb{I_{\text{Class\_Memory}, i}})
  - New Zealand Stamina Heat Sink Override (\Phi_{\text{NZ-Sink}, i}): Models
    the natural cardiorespiratory resistance of New Zealand staying bloodlines
    under elevated temperatures (T_{\text{ambient}} \ge 32^\circ\text{C}):
    \Phi_{\text{NZ-Sink}, i} = \begin{cases} -0.20 & \text{if } \text{Origin}_i \in \{\text{"NZ"}\} \text{ and } T_{\text{ambient}} \ge 32.0^\circ\text{C} \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
  - Two-Year-Old Turf Weight-to-Exposure Drag (\Gamma_{\text{2YO-Turf}, i}):
    Applied strictly to juvenile/2YO races when turf condition is Soft 6 or
    worse (\text{MSCI}_R \le 0.65). Scale the mechanical drag penalty for wide
    draws without cover:
    \Gamma_{\text{2YO-Turf}, i} = 1.50 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{No\_Cover}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \text{MSCI}_R) \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right)
    Where \mathbb{I}_{\text{No\_Cover}, i} = 1 if the juvenile is forced wider
    than two-wide. This represents the extreme energy depletion of unseasoned
    frames on deep turf.
  - Staying Stable Distance Upgrade (\Phi_{\text{SSD}, i}): Applied strictly to
    staying distances (\ge 2000\text{m}) when a horse is stepping up in distance
    by \ge 400\text{m} under the care of a premier long-distance stable
    (Waterhouse/Bott, Waller, Maher). Set a cardiovascular relief credit to
    lower latent frailty:
    \Phi_{\text{SSD}, i} = -0.25 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Elite\_Stayer\_Stable}, i} \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot (W_{\text{eff}, i} - 53.0)}
    This credit offsets the perceived distance risk, recognising that premier
    stables prepare distance runners with superior vascular conditioning.

Phase 3: Sovereign Kinetic Index (SKI) Calculations

Calculate the final Sovereign Kinetic Index (SKI) rating for each remaining
contender: \text{SKI}_i = \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot e^{-(\nu_i - 0.50)}

Automated Data Integrity Sieve (ADIS) Validation Block

Prior to execution of SKI ratings, check if the raw horse database features a
zero-record data-scraping omission. If any required pre-race variable (such as
starting barriers, scratchings, or track states) is missing or unresolved:

  - Flag the missing fields and invoke the Targeted Search Protocol (TSP)
    defined in Phase 4.
  - Once data is successfully retrieved, overwrite the empty placeholders and
    re-run Phase 2.
  - If a zero-record data-scraping omission is detected for historical
    performance:
    \text{If } \sum(\text{Track Starts}) = 0 \text{ AND } \text{Career Starts} > 0: \quad \Theta_{\text{sand}, i} \leftarrow \left( \frac{\text{Career Wins}_i}{\max(1, \text{Career Starts}_i)} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I_{\text{Country\_Trainer}}}
    Overwrite the localised sand specialisation index with career performance
    indices, safeguarding against scraped zero-records.

Pocketing Risk Probability Override (PRP)

To prevent overvaluing inside-drawn horses that lack tactical gate speed and are
vulnerable to being boxed in behind tiring front-runners along the rails,
calculate the pocketing risk:
\text{PRP}_i = \mathbb{I}_{B_{\text{actual}, i} = 1} \cdot \left( 1.0 - \text{ESI}_i \right) \cdot \left[ \sum_{j \in \text{Outer}} \text{ESI}_j \cdot e^{-0.15 \cdot (B_{\text{actual}, j} - 1)} \right]
\text{If } \text{PRP}_i \ge 0.70 \text{ and } \text{PUCL}_i = 0: \quad \text{SKI}_i \leftarrow \text{SKI}_i \cdot (1.0 - 0.20 \cdot \text{PRP}_i)

Gutter Parity Rule (GPR) for Maiden Events

If the race is a Maiden-class event (\text{Class} = \text{"MDN"} or
\text{"MDN-SW"}), calculate the Blended Maiden Index (\text{BMI}) to convert
predictions using the Adaptive Maiden GPR Scaling (\alpha_{\text{dynamic}}):

  - If N_{\text{starts}, i} = 0 (Debutant Uncertainty Anchor):
    \alpha_{\text{dynamic}, i} = \min\left(0.65, 0.45 \cdot \text{MSCI}_R^{-1} \cdot \left( \frac{d_{\text{race}}}{1000} \right)\right)
  - If N_{\text{starts}, i} > 0 (Exposed Maiden):
    \alpha_{\text{dynamic}, i} = 0.70 \cdot ( 1.0 - e^{-0.20 \cdot N_{\text{starts}, i}} )
    \text{SKI}_{\text{Maiden}, i} = (1 - \alpha_{\text{dynamic}, i}) \cdot \text{SKI}_i + \alpha_{\text{dynamic}, i} \cdot \text{CPX}_i \cdot \Theta_{\text{FSSC}, i}
    Where \text{CPX}_i is the Class Parity Exemption rating:
    \text{CPX}_i = \text{Career Places}_i \cdot \left( \frac{\text{AWD}_i}{W_{\text{eff}, i}} \right) \cdot (1.0 + \text{ALSRC}_i)
    Where the Adaptive Local Specialisation Recovery Coefficient (\text{ALSRC})
    is defined as:
