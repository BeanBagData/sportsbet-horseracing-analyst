Phase 1.5: Sequential Elimination and Decision Tree (SEDT) Sieve

To eliminate predictive bias where class-deficient or distance-unsuited runners
are promoted to the top of the ratings, the field must navigate the following
four-stage mathematical sieve before any scoring calculations are processed. If
any runner fails a stage, they are excluded from primary winning consideration
and Top-3 verification.

Stage 1: Silo & Surface Filter

  - Rule: If the track matches a known synthetic venue, or if the track_type is
    Synthetic, immediately force-route the race to the Synthetic_Polytrack silo.
    If the venue matches the VSVG Dirt/Sand registry, force-route the race to
    the Loose_Sand_Dirt silo. Otherwise, route to Turf.

Stage 2: Class & Recent Beaten Margin Sieve (CRMS) & Decoupling

  - Rule: Discard any runner whose average beaten margin in Class 1 or below
    over their last 3 starts exceeds T_{\text{CRMS, adj}} lengths, or who has
    finished worse than 6th in their last 3 starts on any surface (unless
    resuming off a spell, protected by the DDMP, or exempted under the overrides
    below).
  - Formula: Discard if:
    \text{ABM}_{\text{weighted}, i} > T_{\text{CRMS, adj}} \quad \text{or} \quad \max(\text{Pos}_{t-1, i}, \text{Pos}_{t-2, i}, \text{Pos}_{t-3, i}) > 6.0 \cdot (1.0 - \mathbb{I}_{\text{Resuming}, i})
    Where Class-Gradient Decoupling scales the sifting margin limit:
    T_{\text{CRMS, adj}} = 4.5\text{L} \cdot \left(1.0 + 0.15 \cdot \max(0, \text{Class}_{\text{prior}} - \text{Class}_{\text{current}})\right)
  - Exemptions & Overrides to Stage 2 CRMS:
      - Short-Course Decoupling (SCD) Override: For ultra-short sprint races
        (\le 1000\text{ m}), completely decouple and ignore prior uncompetitive
        runs at distances \ge 1200\text{ m} from the Stage 2 CRMS average beaten
        margin calculation.
      - Track-Condition Decoupling (TCD) Override: For turf runners dropping
        back to their pet distance range (1000\text{ m} - 1400\text{ m}) off
        unsuited longer attempts, completely exclude prior Heavy/wet track
        failures over distances \ge 1500\text{ m} from their CRMS average,

      - Outlier Beaten Margin Filter (OBMF): When calculating the 3-start

      - Trial Validity Override (TVO): If a runner has won or placed

Stage 4: Synthetic/Dirt Aptitude Sieve (SAS)

  - Rule: On synthetic or sand/dirt surfaces, a runner must possess a Career
    Surface Place Rate (\text{SPR}) of \ge 15\% to qualify for winning or Top-3
    consideration, unless the runner is a debutant (N_{\text{starts}} = 0) or
    has contested fewer than 2 target surface starts, or
    \text{DDMP\_Active} = \text{True} is active.
    \text{SPR}_i = \frac{\text{Surface Places}_i}{\max(1, \text{Surface Starts}_i)} \quad \text{SAS}_i = \text{Eliminate if } \text{SPR}_i < 0.15 \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Surface\_Starts} \ge 2}
  - Exemptions to Stage 4 SAS:
      - Dynamic Recency Override (DRO): If a runner has competed on today's
        identical track and surface within the last 21 days and finished
        \le 3\text{rd} (beaten \le 2.0\text{ lengths}), they are fully exempt
        from the lifetime SPR requirement of Stage 4.
      - Synthetic Competitiveness Margin (SCM): Exempt a runner from SAS sifting
        if they were beaten by \le 2.0\text{ lengths} in a higher-grade
        synthetic race within 30 days.
      - Low-Sample Surface Exposure Guard (LSSEG): Suspend SAS and p_3 sifting
        for highly-decorated turf class runners making their synthetic debut. If
        N_{\text{synthetic\_starts}} \le 2 and (Career Turf Earnings
        \ge \$100,000 or today's Class Drop Delta \ge 10 rating points):
        \text{Apply Class Resilience Factor: } CRF_i = -0.15 \cdot \ln\left( \frac{\text{Career\_Turf\_Prizemoney}_i}{\$50,000} \right) \quad [\text{deducted from latent frailty } \nu_i]

Phase 2: Dynamic Latent Frailty (\nu_i) and NLS Calibration

Step A: Speed Baseline (NLS) Calibration

00. Deterministic Signature Pattern (DSP) Evaluation

Before executing standard speed baseline calibration, evaluate each active
runner against the following four physical and stable-intent Deterministic
Signature Patterns (DSPs). If a runner matches the boundary conditions, apply
the baseline speed and frailty adjustments directly:

  - DSP-1 (The Local Habituation Loop - Synthetic/Dirt Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Target track is a synthetic or sand/dirt surface
        (\mathbb{I}_{\text{Synthetic/Dirt\_Active}} = 1), runner is trained
        on-course at this venue (\Theta_{\text{home}} = 0.75), and career
        surface place rate \ge 50\% over a minimum of 2 starts.
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 3.50 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.25.
  - DSP-2 (The Effective Mass Advantage - Weight Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Runner carries a light effective weight
        (W_{\text{eff}} \le 55.5\text{ kg}) or achieves an apprentice-assisted
        Effective Mass Advantage
        (\text{EMA}_i = 58.5 - W_{\text{eff}} \ge +3.0\text{ kg}).
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 2.50 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.20.
  - DSP-3 (The Class Gravity Corridor - Class-Drop Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Metropolitan-to-provincial/regional class drop
        active (\text{CMRL}_i = 1) and the runner is prepared by an elite
        metropolitan-grade stable (\Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}} = 0.45).
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 3.00 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.15.
  - DSP-4 (The Fresh-Rail Kinetic Saving - Turf Pivot):
      - Activation Conditions: Track type is Turf, the runner draws an inside,
        ground-saving starting barrier (B_{\text{actual}} \le 4), and running
        style is Leader or On-Pace.
      - Adjustment: \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + 2.00 and
        \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i - 0.30.

1. Spell-Segmented Form Decay & Maiden Exposure Metrics (SSFD & MEDF)

  - Formula:
    \text{FDF}_i = 0.15 \cdot \max(0, \text{Days\_off\_start}_i - 45) \cdot e^{-0.015 \cdot \text{Days\_off\_start}_i}
  - Class Memory Lock (CMRL): If a runner has competed in Group or Listed class
    within the last 180 days, their raw speed capacity is partially insulated
    from long layoffs, capping \text{FDF}_i at a maximum value of 0.05.
  - Maiden Exposure Decay Factor (MEDF): To penalise highly exposed career
    maidens who have hit their physical and psychological development ceiling:
    \text{If } \text{Career Wins}_i = 0 \text{ and } \text{Career Starts}_i \ge 8:
    \text{MEDF}_{\text{NLS}, i} = -1.50 \cdot (\text{Career Starts}_i - 7) \quad (\text{capped at } -15.00)
    \text{MEDF}_{\text{Frailty}, i} = +0.15 \cdot \ln(1 + \text{Career Starts}_i - 7)
    Adjustments:
    \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i + \text{MEDF}_{\text{NLS}, i} \quad \text{and} \quad \nu_{i, \alpha} \leftarrow \nu_{i, \alpha} + \text{MEDF}_{\text{Frailty}, i}

2. Kinetic Muscle & Stamina Modifiers (KMSM)

  - Metropolitan Course-Winner Weight Drop (MCWWD): If a runner has won a
    metropolitan-grade race at the exact scheduled course/venue within their
    last 4 starts and experiences today a carried weight reduction of
    \ge 3.0\text{ kg} relative to that win, apply a speed credit of +3.00 to
    their Final NLS and a credit of -0.15 to their latent frailty (\nu_i).
  - Short-Sprint Match-Fitness Mandate (SSMFM): For short sprints
    (\le 1200\text{ m}), any runner resuming off a spell of \ge 120 days must be
    penalised by a "Neuromuscular Reaction Lag" modifier of +0.15 to latent
    frailty (\nu_i), unless they have won \ge 50\% of their career first-up
    attempts. Conversely, last-start winners within 25 days receive a
    "Peak-Fitness Stride Credit" of -0.10 to latent frailty (\nu_i) and +2.00 to
    Final NLS.
  - Distance-Gate Decoupling Mandate (DGDM): For all races of distance
    \ge 1600\text{ m}, inside barriers (\le 4) receive a "Ground-Saving Traction
    Credit" of -0.10 to their latent frailty (\nu_i), representing ground saved
    over multiple turns.
  - Senior Rider Metropolitan Premium (SRMP): On tight, technical turning
    metropolitan tracks, senior jockeys with a local venue win-rate of \ge 15\%
    receive a "Spatio-Temporal Control Credit" of -0.10 to latent frailty
    (\nu_i) and +1.50 to their Final NLS, mitigating the risk of traffic
    blockages.

3. Trial-to-Race Distance Conditioning Deficit (TRDCD)

To model and penalise horses off a layoff who lack a sufficient aerobic
conditioning foundation for today's race distance:

  - Formula:
    \text{TRDCD}_i = \max\left(0, \frac{d_{\text{race}} - 1.5 \cdot d_{\text{trial}, i}}{d_{\text{race}}}\right) \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.02 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot \mathbb{I}_{t_{\text{lay}} \ge 45}
    Where d_{\text{trial}, i} is the longest recent trial or jump-out distance
    completed within 45 days of today's race, d_{\text{race}} is today's race
    distance, and t_{\text{lay}, i} is the days since the horse's last start.
      - If no trial has been completed within 45 days, set
        d_{\text{trial}, i} = 0, applying the maximum potential TRDCD penalty.
      - Adjustment: \nu_{i, \alpha} \leftarrow \nu_{i, \alpha} + \text{TRDCD}_i

4. Behavioural Greenness Recovery Index (BGRI)

To scale down the behavioural greenness and over-racing penalties for young
horses (2YOs and 3YOs) on their second or third lifetime starts who have been
re-educated via a post-debut trial or jump-out:

  - Formula:
    \text{BGRI}_i = \theta_{\text{greenness}} \cdot \delta_{\text{trial}, i} \cdot e^{-\lambda \cdot (N_{\text{starts}, i} - 1)} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Age} \le 3} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{2 \le N_{\text{starts}} \le 3}
    Where:
      - \theta_{\text{greenness}} = 1.0 if the horse over-raced, hung, or
        finished >8.0\text{L} behind the winner on debut; else 0.50.
      - \delta_{\text{trial}, i} = 0.80 if the horse completed a post-debut
        trial or jump-out of any distance; else 0.00.
      - N_{\text{starts}, i} is the career starts.
      - \lambda = 0.50.
      - Adjustment: \nu_{i, \alpha} \leftarrow \nu_{i, \alpha} - \text{BGRI}_i

5. Metropolitan Training Base Premium (\Lambda_{\text{MTBP}})

To prevent underestimating metropolitan-prepared runners travelling to regional
or provincial circuits, a dynamic training base coefficient is calculated and
applied to the raw NLS baseline:
\Lambda_{\text{MTBP}, i} = \delta_{\text{metro}} \cdot \ln(1 + N_{\text{trials\_30d\_count}}) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Trainer Local Strike Rate}}{10} \right)
Where:

  - \delta_{\text{metro}} = 0.08 if the runner is trained and conditioned at a
    premier metropolitan base (e.g. Eagle Farm, Flemington, Caulfield, Randwick,
    Rosehill, Warwick Farm, Doomben, Morphettville, Ascot); otherwise 0.00.
  - N_{\text{trials\_30d\_count}} is the verified count of barrier trials or
    official jumpouts completed within 30 days of the race.
  - Adjustment:
    \text{Final NLS}_i \leftarrow \text{Final NLS}_i \cdot \left( 1.0 + \Lambda_{\text{MTBP}, i} \right)

6. The Metabolic Engine (M_i)

M_i = \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} + \text{SDF}_{\text{adjusted}, i} + \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} + \Delta_{\text{shock}, i} - \text{CPI}_i + \text{NOETF}_i + \text{SURP}_i - \gamma_{\text{stable}, i} + \text{SVRP}_i + \text{SCCI}_i

  - Asymptotic Metabolic Desynchronisation Index (AMDI):
    \text{AMDI}_i = (1.0 - e^{-0.01 \cdot t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i}}) \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \chi_{\text{specialist}, i})
      - Unified Trial Layoff Restoration Formula (\Phi_{\text{TLR}}): To
        neutralize the laydays cardiorespiratory penalty for horses that have
        been thoroughly conditioned via multiple barrier trials, the effective
        layoff time (t_{\text{eff\_lay}}) is dynamically adjusted:
        \text{If } d_{\text{trial}, i} > 0 \text{ and trial is within } 45\text{ days: } t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i} = t_{\text{lay}, i} \cdot e^{-\left( \lambda_{\text{trial}} \cdot \sum d_{\text{trials\_45d}} / d_{\text{race}} \right)} \quad [\text{where } \lambda_{\text{trial}} = 1.25]
        \text{If } d_{\text{trial}, i} \text{ is unrecorded, but trials have been completed: } t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i} = t_{\text{lay}, i} \cdot e^{-0.50 \cdot \max(0, N_{\text{trial}, i} - 1)}
        \text{Otherwise: } t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i} = t_{\text{lay}, i}
      - Aerobic Base Protection (\text{ABP}_i): If a horse has completed \ge 4
        official trials or jumpouts within 60 days of resuming, set
        \text{ABP}_i = 1. This triggers a negative frailty modifier to offset
        structural second-up decay:
        \Phi_{\text{ABP}, i} = 0.30 \cdot \text{ABP}_i
      - Elite Trial-Conditioned Debutant (ETCD) Override: If a debutant
        (\text{Career Starts}_i = 0) has completed \ge 6 official
        trials/jumpouts within 90 days and is prepared by an elite stable
        (\Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}} = 0.45), protect their cardiovascular base:
        \text{Set } \Delta_{\text{inexp}, i} \leftarrow 0.00 \quad \text{and apply ETCD Credit: } \nu_{i, \alpha} \leftarrow \nu_{i, \alpha} - 0.25 \quad \text{and } t_{\text{eff\_lay}, i} \leftarrow 30.00
      - Anaerobic Distance Scaling (f_{\text{anaerobic}}): Scale down the
        metabolic penalty for short sprints where anaerobic energy pathways
        dominate:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{scaled}, i} = \text{AMDI}_i \cdot \left[ \frac{1}{1 + e^{-0.01 \cdot (d_{\text{race}} - 1150)}} \right]
      - Home-Track Habituation Factor (\Theta_{\text{home}}): Apply ONLY if the
        runner is trained on-course at the exact same venue as the scheduled
        race. If the training track name and scheduled racecourse name do not
        match identically, \Theta_{\text{home}, i} \equiv 0.0. If valid:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{home}, i} = \text{AMDI}_{\text{scaled}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \Theta_{\text{home}}) \quad \text{[where } \Theta_{\text{home}} = 0.75\text{]}
      - Elite-Stable Cardiorespiratory Layoff Discount (\Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}}):
        \Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.45 & \text{if Trainer } \in \{\text{"Ciaron Maher", "Chris Waller", "Ben, Will & JD Hayes", "Anthony & Sam Freedman", "Mick Price & Michael Kent (Jnr)"}\} \text{ and Class } \le \text{Provincial MDN} \\ 1.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \quad \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot \Phi_{\text{ES-CLD}, i}
      - Genetic Aerobic Hyper-Compensation (\Phi_{\text{GAHC}}): Waives resuming
        AMDI penalties for elite first-up performers whose cardiovascular output
        does not decay during layoffs:
        \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i} = \left( \frac{\text{Wins}_{\text{1st-Up}, i}}{\max(1, \text{Starts}_{\text{1st-Up}, i})} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Resuming}, i} \cdot e^{-0.10 \cdot (t_{\text{lay}, i} / 100)} \quad \text{If } \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i} \ge 0.50: \quad \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 - \Phi_{\text{GAHC}, i})
      - Staying Base Exemption (SBE): If
        \text{MaxDistance}_{\text{last 2 starts}} \ge 2400\text{ m}, apply a
        mitochondrial preservation discount:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot 0.25
      - Cardiorespiratory Recovery Decay Factor (CRDF): For horses off layoffs
        without recent conditioning trials, apply a 6.3% raw metabolic capacity
        discount to the adjusted AMDI baseline:
        \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \leftarrow \text{AMDI}_{\text{adjusted}, i} \cdot (1.0 + \alpha_{\text{layoff}} \cdot (1.0 - e^{-0.015 \cdot (t_{\text{lay}, i} - 60)})) \quad \text{[where } \alpha_{\text{layoff}} = 0.20 \text{ if no trials within 45 days, else 0.0]}
  - Dynamic Cardiorespiratory Flat Risk Formula (\text{CRFR}_i): Models first-up
    physical expenditure. If \text{Starts in Prep}_i = 2:
    \text{CRFR}_i = \left(1.0 - e^{-0.008 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot E_{1\text{U}, i} \cdot e^{-\frac{(t_{\text{backup}, i} - 17)^2}{40.5}}
    Where First-Up Stress Intensity (E_{1\text{U}, i}) is:
    E_{1\text{U}, i} = \text{Pace\_Factor}_{1\text{U}, i} \cdot \ln(1 + \text{beaten\_margin}_{1\text{U}, i}) \cdot \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0}
    With \text{Pace\_Factor}_{1\text{U}} = 1.5 (Led/On-Pace), 1.0 (Midfield),
    or 0.6 (Rear).
      - Second-Up Priming Index (\text{SUPI}): If E_{1\text{U}, i} \le 0.60,
        apply a recovery credit:
        \text{SUPI}_i = -0.15 \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-0.05 \cdot t_{\text{lay}, i}}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0}\right) \quad \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i + \text{SUPI}_i
      - Refined Second-Up Deficit Factor (\text{SDF}_{\text{refined}, i}) &
        Cardiorespiratory Progression vs. Recoil (CPVR): If
        E_{1\text{U}, i} > 0.60 and \text{SUPI} conditions are not met, apply
        CPVR routing:
        \text{SDF}_i = (1.0 - \text{ABP}_i) \cdot 0.35 \cdot e^{-0.08 \cdot (t_{\text{backup}, i} - 12)} \cdot \chi_{\text{CPVR}, i}
        Where the CPVR modifier \chi_{\text{CPVR}, i} is:
        \chi_{\text{CPVR}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.45 & \text{if first-up beaten margin } \ge 5.0\text{L (Progression Lane)} \\ 1.35 & \text{if first-up beaten margin } < 3.0\text{L (Recoil Lane)} \end{cases}
      - Youth Super-Compensation Exemption (YSCE): Highly progressive young
        horses hyper-adapt to physical stresses rather than suffering
        performance degradation. If \text{Age}_i = 3, runs this preparation
        \ge 2, and t_{\text{backup}, i} \le 10 days:
        \text{Set } \text{SDF}_i \leftarrow 0.00 \quad \text{and apply Cardiorespiratory Priming Credit: } \nu_{i, \alpha} \leftarrow \nu_{i, \alpha} - 0.20
      - Dynamic Age/Progressive Power Modifier (\Gamma_{\text{prog}}): Scales
        down second-up decay for progressive 3YOs:
        \Gamma_{\text{prog}, i} = e^{-0.15 \cdot \text{Career Starts}_i} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Age}=3} \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Wins}_i}{\text{Career Starts}_i} \right)^{1.5}
      - European Import Hysteresis Exemption (EI-HE): High-class European
        imports are exempted from the SDF penalty:
        \text{If } \text{Sire\_AWD}_i \ge 1800\text{ m and } \text{Origin}_i \in \{\text{"FR", "GB", "IRE"}\}: \quad \text{SDF}_i \leftarrow 0.0
      - Adjusted Second-Up Deficit Factor:
        \text{SDF}_{\text{adjusted}, i} = \text{SDF}_i \cdot \left( 1.0 - \Gamma_{\text{prog}, i} \right)
  - Cardiorespiratory Spaced Layoff Recoil (\text{SLR}_i): Penalises detraining
    for horses with excessive spacing between their first and second runs back:
    \text{If } t_{\text{lay}, i} \ge 150\text{ days and } t_{\text{backup}, i} > 45\text{ days}: \quad \text{SLR}_i = +0.15 \cdot \log_{10}(t_{\text{backup}, i} - 40.0)
  - Cardiorespiratory Recovery Decay Index (\text{CRDI}_i): Vetoes horses on a
    quick backup off an uncompetitive first-up defeat:
    \text{CRDI}_i = \left( 1.0 - e^{-\frac{t_{\text{lay}, i}}{28}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Margin}_{t-1, i}}{2.0} \right) \cdot e^{-\frac{t_{\text{backup}, i}}{10}}
    If \text{CRDI}_i > 1.25: Apply Lactic Rebound Deficit Lock (LRDL). Veto 1st
    Place candidacy status and set: \nu_i \leftarrow \nu_i \cdot \text{CRDI}_i.
  - Metabolic Recoil Modifier (\Delta_{\text{recoil}, i}):
      - Short turnaround peak-performance recoil: If
        t_{\text{lay}} \le 21\text{ days} and \text{CPI}_i = 0.0:
        \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} = 0.15 \cdot \max(0, R_{t-1, i} - R_{\text{avg}, i}) \cdot \left( \frac{21 - t_{\text{lay}}}{21} \right)
      - Second-up peak-win recoil: \Delta_{\text{recoil}, i} = 0.15 if second-up
        off a >120-day spell following a first-up victory, running on a
        \le 21-day backup.
  - Anaerobic Distance Shock Penalty (\Delta_{\text{shock}, i}): Applied to
    stayers dropping back severely in distance off a long layoff:
    \text{If } \Delta_{\text{dist}} = \text{MaxDistance}_{\text{career}} - \text{Distance}_{\text{current}} \ge 600\text{ m and } t_{\text{lay}} \ge 180\text{ days and pedigree AWD} \ge 1800\text{ m}:
    Apply the Anaerobic Distance-Drop Shock Penalty directly to the speed and
    metabolic baseline:
    \Lambda_{\text{class}, i} \leftarrow 0.0 \quad \text{and} \quad \Delta_{\text{shock}, i} = -1.50 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{AWD}_i - 1200}{100} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\Delta_{\text{dist}, i}}{200} \right) \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{sprint}}
  - Neurological Over-Excitation Tension Factor (NOETF): Captures metabolic
    depletion when young horses pull or over-race early:
    \text{NOETF}_i = \mathbb{I}_{\text{Gear\_Change}, i} \cdot e^{-\lambda_{\text{experience}} \cdot N_{\text{starts}, i}} \cdot \Psi_{\text{excitation}, i} \quad [\text{where } \Psi_{\text{excitation}} = 0.35 \text{ represents highly strung sire lines}]
  - Second-Up Recoil Penalty (\text{SURP}_i): Dynamic frailty correction mapping
    structural spell-recoil back into the metabolic engine rather than binary
    sifting. If V_{\text{SR}, i} = 1: \text{SURP}_i = +0.65.
  - Trainer Synthetic 2nd-Up Conditioning Curve (\gamma_{\text{stable}, i}):
    Integrates the trainer-specific second-up peak fitness recovery curve:
    \gamma_{\text{stable}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.15 & \text{if Trainer Synthetic 2nd-Up Strike Rate } \ge 20\% \text{ and } \text{Starts in Prep}_i = 2 \text{ and } \text{track\_type} = \text{"Synthetic"} \\ 0.00 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
  - Senior Veteran Resuming Penalty (\text{SVRP}_i): Compels cardiorespiratory
    depreciation for aging stayers returning off a spell over middle distances:
    \text{If } \text{Age}_i \ge 6 \text{ and } t_{\text{lay}, i} \ge 100 \text{ and } d_{\text{race}} \ge 1400\text{ m:}
    \text{SVRP}_i = +0.15 \cdot (\text{Age}_i - 5) \cdot \left( \frac{t_{\text{lay}, i}}{100} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{d_{\text{race}}}{1400\text{ m}} \right)
  - Somatic Campaign Compression Index (\text{SCCI}_i): Models structural
    campaign-wear and metabolic exhaustion for over-raced runners:
    \text{SCCI}_i = \mu_{\text{fatigue}} \cdot \left( \frac{N_{\text{runs\_30d\_count}}}{3.0} \right)^3 \cdot \ln(1 + \text{beaten\_margin}_{t-1, i})
    Where \mu_{\text{fatigue}} = 0.35 represents the muscle-wear somatic fatigue
    factor.

7. The Mechanical Engine (F_i)

F_i = f_{\text{friction}, i} + D_{\text{barrier}, i} + \text{SEDI}_i + \text{ASST}_i + \text{HTAST}_i

  - Surface-Specific Base Friction (f_{\text{friction}, i}):
    f_{\text{friction}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.15 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{60.0} \right)^2 \cdot (1.0 - \text{EMA}_i \cdot 0.05) & \text{if track\_type is Synthetic (Viscous Wax Binder)} \\ 0.10 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{60.0} \right) & \text{if track\_type is Turf} \\ 0.20 \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{55.0} \right)^2 \cdot (1.0 - \Theta_{\text{sand}, i}) & \text{if track\_type is Sand-Dirt (Plastic Shear)} \end{cases}
  - Barrier-Induced Physical Drag (D_{\text{barrier}, i}): Incorporates the TGCA
    / LRBA track geometry adjustments:
    D_{\text{barrier}, i} = \begin{cases} 0.15 \cdot \left( \frac{B_{\text{actual}, i}}{N_{\text{active}}} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \text{ESI}_i^2) \cdot \left( 1.0 - \frac{\text{run\_to\_first\_turn\_metres} - 200}{400} \right) & \text{if run\_to\_first\_turn\_metres} \ge 300 \\ 0.15 \cdot \left( \frac{B_{\text{actual}, i}}{N_{\text{active}}} \right) \cdot (1.0 - \text{ESI}_i^2) & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}
    Where the run to the turn reduction is capped at a maximum of 50%. Where
    \text{ESI}_i \in [0, 1] is the horse's historical frequency of leading. If
    the field compresses by \ge 25\% (\text{Contraction}_r \ge 0.25), all
    barrier drag penalties are halved for runners with recalculated barriers
    B_{\text{actual}, i} \le 8.
  - Synthetic Experience Deficit Index (\text{SEDI}_i): Progressively penalises
    horses lacking exposure to synthetic tracks:
    \text{SEDI}_i = 0.45 \cdot e^{-0.60 \cdot N_{\text{synthetic\_starts}, i}} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{track\_type="Synthetic"}}
    If Class \le \text{BM56}, scale down \text{SEDI}_i by multiplying by 0.30.
  - Apprentice Synthetic/Dirt Strength Tax (\text{ASST}_i): Models physical
    fatigue of apprentices on high-drag synthetic or deep dirt straight tracks:
    \text{ASST}_i = \mathbb{I}_{\text{Apprentice}} \cdot 0.15 \cdot \text{Claim}_{\text{dynamic}, i} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Synthetic/Dirt\_Active}}
  - Heavy Turf Apprentice Steering/Balance Tax (HTAST): Models the high physical
    difficulty and tactical error rate of apprentices on deep turf:
    \text{HTAST}_i = \mathbb{I}_{\text{Apprentice}} \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{Heavy\_Turf}} \cdot 0.25 \cdot \text{Claim}_{\text{dynamic}, i}
    Where \mathbb{I}_{\text{Heavy\_Turf}} = 1 if track_type is Turf and track
    rating is \ge \text{Heavy 8}, else 0.

8. Biological, Class, and Weight Modifiers

  - Synthetic/Dirt Surface Affinity Factor (\Sigma_{\text{syn}, i}): Quantifies
    past habituation and comfort on non-turf tracks:
    \Sigma_{\text{syn}, i} = 0.40 \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Surface Wins}_i}{\max(1, \text{Surface Starts}_i)} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Surface Starts}_i}{\text{Career Starts}_i} + 0.50 \right) + \text{Habituation\_Credit}_i
    Where \text{Habituation\_Credit}_i = 0.20 if the runner placed 2nd or 3rd at
    their only prior target surface start, otherwise 0.00.
  - Class Drop Elasticity Offset (\Lambda_{\text{class}, i}): Reflects the
    advantage of dropping class. Capped at a maximum credit of -0.15:
    \Lambda_{\text{class}, i} = \min\left(0.15, \ln\left( \frac{\text{Class}_{\text{prior}, i}}{\text{Class}_{\text{current}, i}} \right) \cdot \beta_{\text{track}}\right) \quad [\text{where } \beta_{\text{track}} = 1.5 \text{ for regional/country circuits and 1.0 for metropolitan tracks}]
  - Competitiveness-Scaled Class Offset (C_{\text{offset, refined}, i}): Reduces
    speed decay for metropolitan-grade horses dropping back to

Kinetic Volatility Ingestion (KVI) Integration

To capture pace-sensitive performers (Swoopers/Leaders) who demonstrate elite
winning potential in a single tempo state but are linear-penalised across
others, calculate the pace-tempo standard deviation (KVIF_i):
KVIF_i = \sqrt{\frac{(\text{SKI}_{i, \alpha} - \bar{\text{SKI}}_i)^2 + (\text{SKI}_{i, \beta} - \bar{\text{SKI}}_i)^2 + (\text{SKI}_{i, \gamma} - \bar{\text{SKI}}_i)^2}{3}}
Where:
\bar{\text{SKI}}_i = \frac{1}{3} \left( \text{SKI}_{i, \alpha} + \text{SKI}_{i, \beta} + \text{SKI}_{i, \gamma} \right)

Calculate the Dynamic Volatility Premium (DVP_i) to mathematically elevate fit,
pace-sensitive specialists directly into the priority sequence:
DVP_i = \Psi_{\text{volatility}} \cdot KVIF_i \cdot \mathbb{I}_{\text{fit\_lane}, i}
Where:

  - \Psi_{\text{volatility}} = 0.40 (somatic volatility modifier).
  - \mathbb{I}_{\text{fit\_lane}, i} = 1 if the horse is in the Progression Lane
    (\chi_{\text{CPVR}, i} = 0.45) or has an active preparation
    (t_{\text{backup}, i} \le 25\text{ days}); else 0.00.

Calculate the final, definitive Scenario-Robust SR-SKI*:
\text{SR-SKI}_i^* = \left( 0.60 \cdot \text{SKI}_{i, \alpha} + 0.25 \cdot \text{SKI}_{i, \beta} + 0.15 \cdot \text{SKI}_{i, \gamma} \right) + DVP_i

    L_{\text{reconstruct}, i} = ( \text{Final NLS}_i - \hat{\text{Final NLS}}_i )^2 + 100 \cdot (M_{i, \alpha} - \hat{M}_i)^2 + 100 \cdot (F_i - \hat{F}_i)^2 + 100 \cdot (\nu_{i, \alpha} - \hat{\nu}_i)^2 + (\text{SBEI}_i - \hat{\text{SBEI}}_i)^2
