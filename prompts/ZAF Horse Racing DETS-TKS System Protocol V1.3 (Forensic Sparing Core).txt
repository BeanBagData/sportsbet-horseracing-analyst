Formula 16: Distance-Drop Sparing Index (\text{DDSI}_i)

\text{DDSI}_i = 1.0 + \beta_{\text{drop}} \cdot \ln\left( \frac{\text{Distance}_{t-1}}{\text{Distance}_{\text{today}}} \right) \cdot \left(1.0 - \text{Place\_Rate}_i\right)
where \beta_{\text{drop}} = 0.50, and \text{Place\_Rate}_i is the horse's
historical career placing percentage. This index modifies the Sovereign Kinetic
Index (\text{SKI}_i) directly.

Formula 17: Apprentice Mass-to-Somatic Ratio Sparing (\text{AMSRS}_i)

Measures the mechanical efficiency gained on dry turf by pairing a mature horse
with a high-allowance apprentice, alleviating systemic joint stress:
\text{AMSRS}_i = \left( \frac{W_{\text{carded}, i} - \text{Claim}_i}{W_{\text{carded}, i}} \right) \cdot \left( \frac{\text{Age}_i}{3.5} \right) \cdot \Phi_{\text{maturation}, i}
where lower values represent higher energy-saving potential.

Formula 18: Durbanville Lateral Turn Penalty (\text{CL}_{\text{Durb}, i})

Calculates the centrifugal kinetic energy loss (E_{\text{loss}}) on tight
right-handed turns (turn radius r = 65\text{ m}):
\text{CL}_{\text{Durb}, i} = \frac{v_i^2 \cdot \theta \cdot \left(w_{\text{lane}} \cdot L_{\text{off}, i}\right)}{r \cdot g}
where v_i = 15.5\text{ m/s} (typical Durbanville pace velocity),
w_{\text{lane}} = 1.2\text{ m} (effective lane path width), L_{\text{off}, i} is
the starting barrier lateral offset index (Lane 1 = 0.2; Lane 2 = 1.2; Lane 3
= 2.2), and \theta = 2.356\text{ radians} (135^\circ turn arc).

Formula 18.1: Stable Entry Density Pace Modifier (\text{SED}_i) & Stable Pacing Synergy Index (SPSI)

\text{SED}_{\text{stable}} = \frac{N_{\text{stable\_runners}}}{N_{\text{active}}}
If \text{SED}_{\text{stable}} \ge 0.20, apply a Pace Synergy Bonus
(\Delta \nu_{\text{PSB}} = -0.04) to all runners from that stable.

  - Stable Pacing Synergy Index (SPSI): If a stable has multiple active starters
    (\text{Stable\_Runner\_Count} \ge 2), and one stablemate maps as a pacemaker
    (\text{PSP} == \text{Leader} or \text{Handy}, \text{KGS} \le 1.8) and
    today's wind is a headwind (\text{WS} \ge 10\text{ km/h},
    \theta_{\text{wind}} \le 45^\circ), apply a synergy discount to the trailing
    stablemate, reducing calculated wind-exposure and frailty:
    \Delta\nu_{\text{SPSI}, i} = -0.06 \cdot \text{SED}_{\text{stable}} \cdot \left(\frac{\text{WS} \cdot \cos(\theta_{\text{wind}})}{10}\right)

Formula 19: Pedigree Distance Progression Index (\text{PDPI}_i) & Distance Transition Strain Index (\text{DTSI}_i)

\text{PDPI}_i = \frac{\left(\text{AWD}_{\text{Sire}, i} \cdot 0.65\right) + \left(\text{AWD}_{\text{DamSire}, i} \cdot 0.35\right)}{D_{\text{today}}}

  - Distance Transition Strain Index (\text{DTSI}_i - Formula 19.1): Measures
    the cardiorespiratory strain of step-ups in distance. If
    D_{\text{today}} > D_{\text{last}}, calculate:
    \text{DTSI}_i = \left( \frac{D_{\text{today}} - D_{\text{last}}}{100} \right)^2 \cdot e^{-0.5 \cdot \text{Starts}_i} \cdot \left(1.5 - \text{PDPI}_i\right)
    Apply \text{DTSI}_i directly as an addition to the baseline frailty (\nu_i).
    For unexposed sprinters stepping up to staying trips, DTSI curves upward
    exponentially, modeling rapid lactic acid accumulation.

Formula 20: Inertial Pocket Deceleration Modifier (\text{IPDM}_i), Pocket Trap Probability Factor (PTPF), Shorter Straight Pocket Trap Surcharge (\text{PTPF}_{\text{Inside}}), Pocket Trap Surcharge (PTPS), Maiden-to-Handicap Class Shock Index (MHCSI), & Lateral Velocity Loss (\Delta V_{\text{lat}, i})

  - Inertial Pocket Deceleration Modifier (\text{IPDM}_i):
    \text{If } B_{\text{actual}, i} == 1 \text{ AND } \text{KGS}_i \le 2.0 \implies \Delta\nu_{\text{IPDM}, i} = 0.40 \cdot \left(2.0 - \text{KGS}_i\right) \cdot \text{SML}_i
  - Pocket Trap Probability Factor (PTPF):
    \text{If } B_{\text{actual}, i} \le 2 \text{ AND } \text{PSP}_{\text{leader}} == \text{Leader} \implies \text{PTPF}_i = +0.10 \cdot (3.0 - B_{\text{actual}, i}) \cdot (1.0 - \text{PDPI}_{\text{leader}})
  - Shorter Straight Pocket Trap Surcharge (\text{PTPF}_{\text{Inside}} -
    Formula 20.1): On tight turning courses with a short home straight
    (L_{\text{straight}} \le 500\text{ m}), the pocket risk for rail-drawn
    runners is magnified:
    \text{PTPF}_{\text{Inside}, i} = \text{PTPF}_i \cdot \left(1.0 + \max\left(0.0, \frac{800 - L_{\text{straight}}}{300}\right)\right) \cdot \left(3.0 - B_{\text{actual}, i}\right)
  - Pocket Trap Surcharge (PTPS - Formula 20.2): If a horse has low tactical
    early speed (\text{TSI}_i \le 0.50) and is drawn in Barrier 1 or 2 on a
    turning track, apply:
    \text{PTPS}_i = \lambda_{\text{pocket}} \cdot (1.0 - \text{TSI}_i) where
    \lambda_{\text{pocket}} = 0.10 represents the pocketing friction factor.
  - Maiden-to-Handicap Class Shock Index (MHCSI - Formula 20.3): For any runner
    transitioning from a maiden win to an open handicap:
    \text{MHCSI}_i = 1.0 + \alpha_{\text{shock}} \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i} - 54.0}{54.0} \right) \cdot e^{-\beta_{\text{shock}} \cdot \text{Runs}_i}
    where \alpha_{\text{shock}} = 0.5, \beta_{\text{shock}} = 0.4, and
    \text{Runs}_i is the number of career starts. Apply the resulting value as a
    direct multiplier to the baseline latent frailty (\nu_i).
  - Lateral Velocity Loss Correction (\Delta V_{\text{lat}, i} - Formula 20.4):
    Quantify the velocity penalty incurred when a runner must shift laterally to
    escape traffic:
    \Delta V_{\text{lat}, i} = -1.0 \cdot \left( V_{\text{mid}} \cdot \sin(\theta_{\text{switch}}) \right) \cdot \left( \frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{54.0} \right)
    where V_{\text{mid}} = 15.5\text{ m/s} and \theta_{\text{switch}} = 15^\circ
    (\sin(15^\circ) = 0.2588).

Formula 21: Somatic Compaction-Tendon Interaction (SCTI)

Model hoof-to-turf interaction on firm turf using a parabolic strain-energy
density curve to refine the Elastic Sparing Recoil Engine (ERSE):
\text{SCTI}_i = \left(\frac{W_{\text{eff}, i}}{54.0}\right)^{1.8} \cdot \left(1.0 - e^{-2.5 \cdot \text{Compaction\_State}}\right)
where \text{Compaction\_State} = 1.0 if the track rating is "Good" or "Firm"
(0.0 otherwise). If \text{SCTI}_i \ge 1.15 on dry compacted turf
(\text{Compaction\_State} == 1), apply the Tendon Sparing Recoil bonus
\Delta\nu_{\text{recoil}} = -0.10 to represent peak kinetic conservation. If
W_{\text{eff}, i} \le 55.0\text{ kg}, the elastic tendon recoil is insufficient,
and a Muscular Fatigue Tax of \Delta\nu_{\text{stamina}} = +0.08 is applied.

Formula 22: Centrifugal Slipstream Preservation (CSPI) & Turn-Radius Centrifugal Tax (TRCT)

  - Centrifugal Slipstream Preservation Index (CSPI - Formula 22.1): On tight
    turns, reward lightweight draft-shielded runners:
    \text{CSPI}_i = S_{\text{draft}, i} \cdot \left(\frac{54.0}{W_{\text{eff, SML}, i}}\right)^{1.5} \cdot \left(\frac{100}{R_{\text{turn}}}\right)
    Add \text{CSPI}_i directly to the Net Thermodynamic Conservation Index
    (\text{NTCI}_i) score.
  - Turn-Radius Centrifugal Tax (TRCT - Formula 22.2): Compute the non-linear
    kinetic energy lost rounding a bend:
    \text{TRCT}_i = \left(\frac{v_{\text{horse}}^2 \cdot \theta \cdot w_{\text{lane}} \cdot B_{\text{actual}, i}}{R_{\text{turn}} \cdot g}\right) \cdot \left(1.0 - S_{\text{draft}, i}\right)
    where v_{\text{horse}} = 15.5\text{ m/s}, \theta = 2.356\text{ radians}
    (135^\circ turn), w_{\text{lane}} = 1.2\text{ m} (effective lane path
    width), and g = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2. If TRCT \ge 0.04, apply a frailty penalty
    of \Delta\nu_{\text{turn}} = +0.05 to model micro-slip traction loss.

Phase 4: Sovereign Kinetic Index (SKI) Engine

Section 1: Baseline Ingestion & Metrical Anchors

To prevent static form traps, all mathematical evaluations of raw capacity are
consolidated into the Sovereign Kinetic Index. This continuous efficiency index
acts as a baseline "Raw Engine Capacity" index (\text{SKI}_i), which is
subsequently scaled to compute the Net Thermodynamic Conservation Index
(\text{NTCI}_i).

Section 2: Raw Nominal Latent Speed (Raw NLS) & Veteran Decay

Calculate base speed potential:

1.  Base Scalar: Initialize with a standard baseline of 80.
2.  Career Class Adjustments: Add up to +10 based on career win/place
    percentages and class competitiveness. Deduct up to -10 for high-mileage,
    non-competitive profiles (Win Rate <5%).
3.  Recent Performance Peak: Add up to +5 for strong closing sectionals or
    close-margin finishes (<1.5\text{L}) in higher grades.
4.  Ex-Import Class Drop Override (PCGM): Apply Regional Gravity Shift Modifier
    (PCGM): If
    \text{Circuit\_prior} \in \{\text{Kenilworth}, \text{Durbanville}\} AND
    \text{Circuit\_current} \in \{\text{Turffontein}, \text{Vaal}, \text{Fairview}, \text{Greyville}\} \implies \text{LPP}_i = \text{LPP}_i + 6.0.
    Western Cape and Gauteng form is superior to Eastern Cape and KZN form;
    Cape/Gauteng drop-downs possess an intrinsic Class Gravity Delta (+6.0 NLS)
    that overrides synthetic debut or long layoff penalties.
5.  Synthetic-Turf Decoupling: If today's track is Synthetic (Silo D), decouple
    turf form entirely:
    \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{synthetic}} = \left(0.70 \cdot \text{NLS}_{\text{synthetic\_historic}}\right) + \left(0.30 \cdot \text{NLS}_{\text{recent\_overall}}\right)
6.  Castration Hormonal Recoil Adjustment: Multiply Raw NLS by \text{PGRF}_i
    (Formula 53).
7.  Detraining Aerobic Decay Adjustment (ZA-JTEM): Deduct \text{CRDI}_i
    (Formula 54) directly from Raw NLS:
    \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{calibrated}, i} = \text{Raw NLS}_i \cdot \text{PGRF}_i - \text{CRDI}_i
    Note: \text{CRDI}_i is completely nullified if ZA-JTEM is triggered.
8.  Age-Decay Acceleration Factor (ADAF): If horse \text{Age} \ge 6 years, scale
    down the calibrated speed rating to correct for veteran muscle contraction
    decline:
    \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{calibrated}, i} = \text{Raw NLS}_{\text{calibrated}, i} \cdot \left(1.0 - 0.025 \cdot \left(\text{Age}_i - 5\right)\right)
9.  Distance-Drop Sparing Index (DDSI): Apply \text{DDSI}_i (Formula 16)
    directly to the calibrated Raw NLS to adjust for trip drop-downs.

  - Formula D: Somatic Exposure & Hardiness Filter (\text{SEHF}_i):
    \text{If } S_i < 10 \implies \text{EDP}_i = -3.0 \cdot \left(10 - S_i\right); \text{ if } S_i \ge 10 \implies \text{EDP}_i = 0.0
    \text{NTCI}_{\text{final}, i} = \text{NTCI}_{\text{base}, i} + \text{DSB}_i + \text{EDP}_i
    Apply Centrifugal Slipstream Preservation Index CSPI_i (Formula 22.1)
    directly to \text{NTCI}_{\text{final}, i} if active on tight turns.
  - Formula E: Net Kinetic Potential (\text{NKP}_{\text{isolated}, i}):
    \text{NKP}_{\text{isolated}, i} = W_{\text{alloc}, i} - 3.5 \cdot \text{Drag Penalty}_i \quad \text{where } \text{Drag Penalty}_i = W_{\text{eff, SML}, i} \cdot \left(1.0 - \text{Compaction\_State}\right)
  - Formula F: Thermodynamic Kinetic Surplus (\text{TKS}_i):
    \text{TKS}_i = \frac{\text{NTCI}_{\text{final}, i} - \text{NTCI}_{\text{field\_mean}}}{\sigma_{\text{NTCI}}}
